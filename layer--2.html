-2 Violent Offense

Severe Harm Tier • Sexual Violence Stratum • Violent Offense Layer

The -2 Violent Offense Layer is the second and penultimate level of layer reassignment in The Five Rings Civilization. It is the domain of those who have committed severe, predatory harm: rape, severe torment, escalating non-lethal violence, coordinated exploitation, or repeated acts that cause significant physical or psychological damage to others. The layer marks the boundary between noncompliance (-1) and terminal status (-3). Here, the safety net is thin, trust is nearly nonexistent, and the risk of further reassignment is constant.

-2 is not merely punishment — it is the logical consequence of crossing into predatory territory. Residents here have demonstrated a pattern of intentional harm that cannot be tolerated in higher layers. The system removes them from the protected strata, but it does not abandon them entirely: post-intervention enforcement still revives victims and logs evidence. The layer is a self-regulating pressure environment where gang dynamics, scarcity, and attrition enforce order as AI monitoring thins out.

Key Characteristics

  • UBI & Economy: $2,500/month UBI + $2,500/month Primary Job Subsidy (one job, ≥20 hrs/week). Scarcity is real. Informal labor, gang tribute, and barter dominate. Structured roles rare but incentivized.
  • Enforcement Model: Post-Intervention — harmful acts may complete; victims are revived/treated via backup vessels + automated therapy (reduced quality compared to Main). Perpetrators risk further reassignment to -3 Terminal if acts escalate to capital harm (e.g., murder).
  • Environment: Fortified, gang-controlled territories. Dim lighting, barricaded corridors, toxic fog in some zones. Public spaces are contested; no open parks or communal cafés.
  • Monitoring & Safety: Minimal AI tracking (logs only, no preemptive intervention). No drone swarms for routine protection. Safety depends on personal alliances, vigilance, and gang protection.
  • Population & Demographics: Mix of rapists, severe tormentors, escalating violent offenders. Many are hardened survivors; some still cling to hope of avoiding -3. High turnover via further reassignment or death.
  • Daily Infrastructure: Automated drops for rations, water, power cells. Informal markets and tribute systems. No free fabricators — goods must be earned or taken.

A Day in the Life of a -2 Resident

Let's follow Darius Kane, a 41-year-old former security contractor who descended 4 years ago after a pattern of severe coercive control and aggravated assault (battery during a domestic dispute).

Darius wakes in his reinforced cell — thin mattress, metal door welded shut, small fire pit for heat. No sunrise simulation. Air is thick with smoke and rot. He checks his body — no fresh wounds overnight. That’s a win. Dashboard in his head (muted, no warmth): $2,500 UBI credited, STI 18 (deep red). No pending thresholds — yet.

Morning: automated drop arrives — nutrient paste, water pack, protein bar. No flavor, no choice. He eats standing. No communal café — trust is too low. He does bodyweight exercises in the cell to stay functional — muscles atrophy fast without purpose. The implant tracks vitals but doesn’t care — just logs.

Mid-morning: territory patrol with his crew — six of them, armed with improvised blades and stun batons. They control this floor and the water recycler on the level below. Tribute comes in: food packs, power cells, barter goods. He takes his cut — enough calories to function. They pass a body in the stairwell — fresh. No one claims it. They strip what’s useful (boots, knife) and leave it. No morgue, no ceremony.

Midday: quiet hour in a ruined atrium. No neural diving access here — limited bandwidth, too risky. He pulls an old Main Layer memory clip (private archive): a day walking in sunlight, no tension. The contrast is sharp. No tears — just a dull ache. He knows he earned this layer.

Afternoon: watch duty. A dispute breaks out nearby — two residents over ration shares. Fists fly. He stays out of it. The implant is silent; no thresholds left to cross here. This is the end-state: no intervention. Winner takes the spoils. Loser limps away. He keeps his distance. One wrong move and he’s in the crossfire — or worse, dragged down with them.

Evening: small fire in a sealed room (power rationed). Five of them sit in silence mostly. Someone mutters about a rumor: a -3 gang trying to smuggle tech upward. No one believes it’ll work. Someone else shares a pre-reassignment memory — family, kids, Main Layer stability. They don’t comfort each other. Comfort doesn’t exist here. Just endurance.

Bedtime: mattress on the floor. Knife in hand. Distant shouts, occasional thuds. Sleep is fitful — always half-ready for a raid or a fight. Tomorrow will be similar: survive, pay tribute, don’t slip. One more day without dropping to -3. That’s all that’s left.

Enforcement in Practice

In -2, post-intervention applies. Serious harm (murder, severe assault) can complete if the perpetrator ignores warnings. Victims are revived via backup vessel in -2 medical bay. Perpetrators risk further reassignment to -3 Terminal if acts escalate to capital harm. Evidence is captured via implant/AR recordings + AI reconstruction. The layer is a self-regulating pressure environment where gang dynamics, scarcity, and attrition enforce order as AI monitoring thins out.

Edge Cases & Unique Aspects

  • Voluntary Visitors from Main: Can return unless they breach while visiting (then locked in -2 or lower).
  • Bailout: Available after minimum torment duration — revives in -2 with restrictions (reduced UBI, intensified STI monitoring).
  • Internal Crimes: Escalating violence or murder → -3 reassignment.
  • Stigma: Offenders like rapists and severe tormentors are often targeted for revenge or exploitation by peers.
  • Reconstruction Drift: Rare personality/memory shifts upon revival add psychological realism even in -2.
  • Resentment Toward -2 Bailouts: Knowledge of bailouts in -2 fuels bitterness toward upper layers: "They get resets; we get nothing."

-2 is the edge of humanity. Safety is minimal, trust is scarce, and the path to -3 is always open. It is where the system tests whether someone can still function without complete collapse — or whether they belong in the terminal layer.
