-2 Violent Offense

Severe Harm Tier  ·  Post-Intervention Enforcement

-2 Violent Offense is the second tier of reassignment in The Five Rings — the layer for those who have committed serious predatory harm: rape, severe assault, systematic exploitation, or patterns of coercive violence that crossed the thresholds that -1 does not cover. The safety infrastructure that characterizes Main Layer and -1 is substantially thinner here. AI monitoring continues to log and to restore victims, but active protection has largely withdrawn. The environment is shaped less by the system and more by the people the system has placed in it.

Overview & Purpose

The design logic of -2 follows directly from VMSS's core framework: harmful behavior permanently changes the environment a person is permitted to inhabit. Citizens assigned here have demonstrated predatory conduct — not the accumulated low-harm record of -1, but acts that directly and severely damaged other people. The system's response is not incarceration and not rehabilitation programming. It is environmental consequence. The layer reflects the level of trust the civilization is willing to extend to its residents, calibrated precisely to the conduct that brought them there.

Victims are still restored in -2. The system has not abandoned the principle that innocent people — including those who encounter harm within this layer — deserve restoration where it is possible. What the system has withdrawn is the presumption that residents here deserve the same level of ambient protection as those who have not committed predatory acts.

Key Characteristics

  • Economy: $2,500/month UBI plus $2,500/month Primary Job Subsidy for structured qualifying work — rare but available for maintenance, security coordination, and resource management roles. Barter, informal labor, and gang-controlled tribute systems dominate actual exchange in most districts.
  • Enforcement Model: Post-Intervention. Harmful acts may complete. Victims are revived or treated, though response quality and speed are reduced compared to Main Layer. Murder within -2 triggers immediate reassignment to -3 Terminal.
  • Environment: Fortified and contested. Residential districts are gang-controlled territories. Public infrastructure is maintained at minimal functional levels — lighting is dim, corridors are narrow, common spaces are occupied rather than open. The physical environment reflects the trust deficit of its population.
  • Monitoring: Logging only, no preemptive intervention, no routine drone protection. The implant continues to record intent and events. The record follows residents for the rest of their time in VMSS, including if they eventually face further reassignment to -3.
  • Population: Residents assigned for rape, severe assault, escalating coercive violence, and similar predatory conduct. High turnover — further reassignment to -3 removes a significant portion of the population over time. Social order is maintained through territorial control rather than civic infrastructure.

A Day in the Life of a -2 Resident

Callum Reese has been in -2 for three years. He was reassigned from Main Layer after a pattern of severe domestic violence that escalated to aggravated assault on two separate occasions. The system had logged both incidents in full. He knew the second one would trigger reassignment; he did it anyway. He does not think about that decision much anymore.

He wakes in a reinforced single unit — two rooms, basic furniture, a door that locks from both sides. The automated drop slot delivered rations overnight: two meal packs, a water unit. He eats without fabrication, without choice. His dashboard runs muted: $2,500 credited, STI 14. He checks the floor monitor — a shared network among the eight-person crew he has been affiliated with for the past eighteen months. No overnight incidents on their territory. He notes it as a productive outcome and begins his day.

The crew's informal role in this district is resource management — they control access to two water recyclers and a power cell distribution point on floors twelve through fifteen. It is not a legitimate occupation. It is the only structure available in a layer where legitimate structure has largely been replaced by territorial logic. The work involves presence, negotiation, and occasional physical assertion of boundaries. Callum handles the negotiation side. He is good at reading people and he has learned that -2 runs on credibility more than force — force is expensive and attracts attention from the remaining monitoring infrastructure.

Midday is quiet. He eats, reads whatever archived content is accessible on the limited-bandwidth terminal in the common area, and does not go looking for conflict. Most of his energy management in -2 is about not escalating situations that do not need to escalate. The implant logs everything, and he is aware that a single murder in this layer ends whatever is left of his record permanently. That awareness keeps him calibrated in a way the layer's social pressure alone would not.

Evening: the crew gathers. A dispute between two residents two floors down resolved without his involvement. He registers that as a positive. Someone mentions a rumor about a new arrival — fresh reassignment from -1, looks disoriented, not yet affiliated. He listens without committing to anything. In the morning he will decide whether to approach or leave it alone.

He sleeps in a layer where what remains of the system's protection is a thin membrane between him and further consequence. He put himself here. He understands that with a clarity that was not available to him in Main Layer, where the consequences were always slightly abstract. Here they are immediate and structural. He does not experience this as a revelation. He experiences it as a fact about where choices lead.

Enforcement in Practice

Post-intervention applies in -2. Acts of violence may complete. Victims are restored where backup vessel infrastructure is still functional — response quality has degraded compared to higher layers, but the system continues to log every incident in full. Murder is the hard threshold: a single capital act triggers immediate reassignment to -3, with no further process. The record from -2 accompanies the resident. It cannot be cleared.

Edge Cases & Unique Aspects

  • Bailout: Available after minimum residency duration. Revival occurs in -2 with reduced UBI and intensified monitoring. Bailout does not alter layer status or prior record.
  • Stigma Dynamics: Certain offense categories — sexual violence, exploitation of children — create significant social risk within -2 itself. Gang dynamics and vigilante logic in this layer are not neutral. Residents with particular offense histories often face targeting from peers.
  • Reconstruction Drift: Backup vessel revival in -2 carries a higher drift risk than Main Layer — typically 1 to 2%. The compounding effect of multiple revivals in a lower layer is documented but not preventable.
  • Resentment Toward Upper Layers: Awareness of -1 bailout provisions and Main Layer conditions generates persistent resentment in -2, particularly regarding the asymmetry between what different tiers restore after death. This resentment reinforces group solidarity within crews and subcultures and occasionally manifests as attempts to breach upward — almost all of which fail.
  • Path Forward: There is no established reintegration path from -2 back to Main Layer. Some residents maintain low-violation records for extended periods and their cases may be reviewed, but the threshold for upward reassignment from -2 is substantially higher than for -1, and it is not guaranteed by conduct alone.

-2 is the system's clearest statement that predatory harm carries permanent environmental cost. The restoration infrastructure is still present — victims still matter here. What is gone is the presumption that a predatory actor deserves the same ambient protection as the people they harmed.
