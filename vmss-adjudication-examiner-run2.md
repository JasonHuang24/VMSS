# VMSS Adjudication Examiner Report — 20 Scenarios Against the Corpus in Force

**Corpus:** charter.html (Tier 1) · laws.html (Consolidated Code, Tiers 1–4 incl. Founding Corpus) · law-polling.html (Enactment Register, LP-001–LP-079)
**Rate schedule in force:** 50 / 25 / 12.5 / 6.25 above $10M, effective 2295 (LP-074, certified 2294). All scenarios set 2305–2306 unless noted.
**Method:** every ruling cites the controlling instrument first, supporting envelopes after. Gap claims require a ≥3-envelope sweep. The corpus's hedges are ruled as hedges, not bright lines.

## PART I — ORGANIC SCENARIOS

### S1 [ORGANIC] — Thirty Hours at the Fabrication Line
**Scenario:** Petra Voll, 34, Main Layer, holds a qualifying job as a fabrication-proxy maintenance technician — critical infrastructure under Article III.II — at 20 hours/week, drawing the $10,000/month Primary Job Subsidy on top of UBI. Her employer, a mid-sized vessel-components firm, wins a surge contract and asks her for 30 hours/week for six months at her agreed base wage of $40/hour. Petra accepts. At month's end the firm's payroll system pays base wage only for all 30 hours. Petra files through the standard civic channel. The firm's operations lead argues the extra ten hours were "voluntary surge participation," not employer-requested overtime, and separately floats restructuring her role into two 15-hour contracts held by two affiliated entities to avoid the issue going forward.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-076, The Overtime Premium Protocol** (Federal, 2299) — controlling. "In Main Layer, the monthly subsidy is $10,000 — weekly value $2,500 — implying an overtime rate of $125 per hour beyond the qualifying threshold... An employer requesting 30 hours of qualifying work in Main Layer owes $1,250 in overtime on top of whatever the agreed base wage is."
2. **Charter Article III.II** — fixes the entitlement and derivation: "Every hour of qualifying work beyond 20 per week triggers the Overtime Premium Protocol: the employer owes the worker the layer's primary subsidy rate indexed by the hour... paid out of pocket — not funded by the civilization."
3. **The Individual Attribution Act** (Founding Corpus) — "VMSS does not recognize corporate personhood or collective liability... the system evaluates every person in the decision chain individually."
4. **Article XVIII** — coordination detection across affiliated entities.

**Ruling:** Petra is owed $125/hour for each hour beyond 20 per week — $1,250 per 30-hour week on top of base wage, per LP-076's own worked example. The "voluntary surge" framing fails: III.II attaches the premium to hours of qualifying work beyond 20, not to the label the employer puts on the request; the implant ledger's record of the hours worked is dispositive. The two-entity restructuring fails twice over: III.II conditions the subsidy on "one qualifying job of 20 or more hours per week," so the split would strip Petra of her $10,000/month subsidy (making it self-defeating for her to accept), and the Individual Attribution Act plus Article XVIII pierce the affiliated-entity structure — the payroll decision-makers carry the evasion on their own ledgers.

**Seams exercised:** Charter III.II (entitlement + derivation) → LP-076 (rate at federal tier, post-LP-076 relocation via the Enabling Consolidation Amendment) → Founding Corpus attribution. Meshed cleanly — the 2299 relocation left the derivation recoverable from retained Charter text exactly as designed.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether the split-contract maneuver would also draw an affirmative sanction (vs. merely failing), since no statute names contract-splitting; the attribution envelope reaches the actors but the offense classification would be first-instance.

### S2 [ORGANIC] — The Forty-Seven-Day Gap
**Scenario:** Marta Iyeru, 61, Main Layer, is killed instantly when a cargo hauler running well above the LP-016 corridor limit (110 km/h) strikes her transit pod. Her implant is intact; her last backup-vessel sync was 47 days ago — inside LP-038.2's 90-day notification window, so no lapse flag exists. The hauler's operator, Denz Okafor, also Main Layer, had disabled his vehicle-assist and was steering manually while impaired. Medical drones arrive in seconds but Marta is beyond field stabilization. The enforcement response classifies the fatality as negligent homicide. Marta's family asks: does she come back, from when, and who pays?

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-065, Victim Continuity Restoration Act** (Federal, 2208) — controlling. "Where the enforcement response classifies a fatality as criminal homicide by another, the responding unit performs a terminal sync capture: the victim's mind-state is captured through the implant into enforcement holding infrastructure, and emergency fabrication follows under LP-022 queue precedence. The victim returns to their pre-crime state."
2. **The Enforcement Chain** (Founding Corpus) — fixed sequence: implant records, drones deploy, revival initiates, perpetrator identified through telemetry and reassigned on severity.
3. **The Medical Response Standard** (Founding Corpus) — "no insurance, no triage and no economic barrier at the point of response."
4. **Charter III.V + Harmed-Party Restoration Standard** — restoration costs satisfied from the perpetrator's liquidation share before treasury absorption.
5. **Article XXVI** — impairment "weighs as a contextual factor in assessing culpability — not as a mitigating exit from consequence."

**Ruling:** Because the death is classified criminal homicide, LP-065's terminal sync capture — not the 47-day-old backup — is the restoration source: Marta returns to her pre-crime state, not to a self missing seven weeks. (Had this been a no-fault accident, she would have revived automatically from the stale sync under the Continuity Integrity Act — "a citizen who dies without removing the implant is revived automatically at full fidelity" — carrying the 47-day memory gap; LP-065 is an enforcement remedy, not an upgrade to ordinary mortality.) Okafor: impaired operation causing death is evaluated on the Article XIV axes with impairment as context; negligent homicide routes to -2 under LP-065's own severity framework. His assets liquidate at 100% under III.V involuntary descent; Marta's restoration cost and restitution are paid from his share before the treasury absorbs the remainder. Her record is annotated victimized with no trust penalty.

**Seams exercised:** LP-065 → LP-022 (queue) → Continuity Integrity Act (binary revival semantics) → III.V liquidation priority → Article XXVI impairment doctrine. Meshed cleanly; the criminal/ordinary fork in restoration source is the load-bearing hinge and both branches are textual.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether negligent (vs. reckless) vehicular death always clears LP-065's "criminal homicide" classification threshold, since the multi-factor evaluation could in principle read a pure-negligence case below it, reverting Marta to stale-sync revival.

### S3 [ORGANIC] — The Third Child
**Scenario:** Ana and Ravi Toma, Main Layer, both 40, have two children and a combined inflow of $52,000/month: $20,000 UBI, $20,000 dual Primary Job Subsidies, $12,000 earned. In 2305 their third child is born. They knew the schedule — Article XXVII says the incentive structure is "fully legible: every citizen knows the tax schedule before conceiving" — but want a third child anyway. Six months later they are startled that their savings, previously untouched by the Savings Circulation Mandate because their district's aggregate sat below the $100B trigger, are now being garnished every month regardless. They also ask whether the new baby's UBI can offset the squeeze.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-064, Replenishment Assessment and Child Dividend Stewardship Act** (Federal, 2206; rate received 2299) — controlling. "The escalation compounds at 50% per child beyond the threshold. The baseline aggregate effective rate is 40%... The third child raises it to 60%." Measured "against total parental inflow: earned income, UBI, and Primary Job Subsidy combined."
2. **Charter Article XXVII** — architecture: dividend never withheld at source; shortfall accrues as replenishment liability collecting through the debt-deadline-reassignment mechanism; "Crossing the threshold also detaches the household's savings circulation mandate from its district trigger — it operates household-attached at the escalated rate for as long as the escalation applies."
3. **Charter III.III** — UBI/PJS untaxed at source, unconditionally.
4. **LP-064 provision (3) + Article XXVII** — "A minor's UBI accrues to a child-titled account stewarded jointly by the parents and the child's independent legal advocate... drawdowns must attribute to the child's benefit through the implant ledger."

**Ruling:** The Tomas' scheduled rate is now 60% of $52,000/month. Their UBI and PJS arrive untouched (III.III absolute); the escalated instruments recover what they can from the $12,000 earned stream, and the shortfall accrues as replenishment liability with a Meritboard-set repayment deadline — unpaid, it ends in -1 reassignment orders for the parents. The SCM household-attachment is not an error: crossing the threshold detached their household from the district trigger, so the mandate now pulses against their balance continuously at the escalated rate. The baby's $10,000/month cannot offset anything: it accrues to a child-titled account, benefit-attributed via the implant ledger, vesting at 18 — "reproduction adds no parent-capturable income at any family size." All three children are held harmless in every branch.

**Seams exercised:** Charter XXVII (architecture) → LP-064 (rate + measurement base + stewardship) → III.III (untaxed absolute) → III.VII (SCM detachment). Meshed cleanly — LP-064 exists precisely because the pre-2206 seam (dividend-living households with no taxable footprint) had inverted the deterrent, and the register records the closure.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure of the exact month the household-attached SCM begins (at birth vs. at first assessment cycle), which no instrument states.

### S4 [ORGANIC] — The Artist Who Moved Down
**Scenario:** Joss Ferren, 29, Main Layer graphic artist earning irregular market income (non-qualifying work, no subsidy), files for elective residency in a -1 casino district in 2305 — cheaper living, a looser speed-and-noise culture, and legal speculative markets, which are excluded from the upper layers. He transfers $50,000 of his Main savings at the gate. Over two years he day-trades -1 equities successfully, accumulating a substantial compliance-token balance, and wonders: can he bring the winnings home when he returns? Meanwhile his Main-side savings sit untouched. One night a drunk local swings at him outside a casino; Joss drops him with one punch and walks away.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter III.V (Elective Residency)** — controlling for status: "Citizens may elect residency in any lower layer without relinquishing origin-layer status. Origin-layer assets remain untouched... Elective residents remain subject to the enforcement posture of their origin layer — this is a legal classification, not an operational guarantee... Elective residents may return to their origin layer at any time."
2. **The Central Banking Authority** (Founding Corpus, schedule received 2299) — "Visitors and elective residents transfer origin-layer assets on the same progressive scale — 10% retained on the first $1M... converted at destination-layer purchasing power." III.IV gradient: -1 at ~1.3–1.8x.
3. **Charter III.IV** — "Upward conversion is prohibited without exception."
4. **The Layer Provisioning Schedule** (Founding Corpus) — "speculative markets are permitted beginning at -1... alongside a thinner commercial regulatory overlay and full charter-floor enforcement on physical safety."
5. **LP-047.3** (Pillar) — visitor/elective-resident defensive force: one force-tier above the attacker's, temporal scope, narrow provocation definition.
6. **Charter III.VII** — -1 SCM: district-aggregate trigger ($50B per LP-070), 5%/month on total savings.

**Ruling:** The $50,000 transfer retains $5,000 through the schedule, issued as roughly 3,350 compliance tokens at midpoint settlement (the Charter's own illustration: $10,000 retained ≈ 6,700 tokens). His trading is lawful (-1 is the first layer down where speculation is legal) and his winnings are -1-siloed permanently: upward conversion is prohibited without exception — the tokens stay in the -1 economy whether he does or not. His Main assets are untouched and accessible on return (III.VI). His -1 balance is exposed to -1's SCM on total savings when the district trigger fires. The punch: attacked at unarmed-physical tier (tier 2), he responded within tier — as an elective resident below his origin layer he holds LP-047.3 protection outright, and defense ended when the threat ended. His origin-layer (Main) enforcement posture evaluated the act; no consequence attaches.

**Seams exercised:** III.V status mechanics → Central Banking settlement → III.IV silo wall → Layer Provisioning market legality → LP-047.3 defensive shield. Meshed cleanly. The "legal classification, not an operational guarantee" hedge was preserved: Joss gets Main thresholds, not Main drone density.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether -1 SCM reads an elective resident's balance into the -1 district aggregate or attributes by registered home address to his Main district (III.VII: "registered home address as recorded on each citizen's implant ledger"), which turns on where his address of record moved at election.

### S5 [ORGANIC] — Nia Chooses Main
**Scenario:** Nia, 9, was born in a -2 cooperative territory to parents who run a salvage-brokerage stall. Her life is not violent — her parents are careful people in a rough economy — but at school-equivalent sessions run by the cooperative she learns to read from a civic-education terminal, and at some point pieces together from other children that she can leave. She tells her AI legal advocate — assigned at birth, as to every child — that she wants to go to Main Layer. Her father, furious, tells her she may not, forbids her from speaking to "the box" again, and takes her terminal access away for a month. Her mother quietly asks the advocate what happens next.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article VIII** — controlling. "The right to relocate to Main Layer (0) is a standing human right available to every child at any age, enforceable through the implant-linked legal mediation system without requiring parental consent... every child has access to an independent AI legal advocate from birth."
2. **LP-068, Neonatal Relocation Facilitation Act** (Federal, 2217) — advocate execution channel: "a child's Article VIII advocate holds explicit procedural standing to execute relocation for any child who assents, at any age and any expressive capacity the child can meet — execution of the child's own right, never advocate-initiated removal."
3. **The Civic Floor Act** (Founding Corpus) — federally facilitated child relocation is non-withdrawable floor content at every layer, including -2.
4. **The Clean-Record Doctrine** (Founding Corpus) — "a parent who coerces a child out of exercising it is evaluated on the standard axes as a denial of a charter right to a minor"; also: "The system does not proactively inform children of the relocation right — discovery is organic."
5. **LP-023 + LP-034** — autoparenting facility standards; uniform educational floor.
6. **Article XXVII / LP-064** — the child's full Main-rate UBI to a child-titled account.

**Ruling:** Nia's assent to her advocate is sufficient: the advocate executes the relocation through the federally facilitated channel, no parental consent required, no fee, and the father's prohibition has no legal effect — "Parents in lower layers hold no institutional dominion over their children's right of exit" (Art. VIII). She arrives at a Main autoparenting facility under LP-023 standards with a clean record inheriting nothing from her parents' ledgers, full $10,000/month UBI accruing to her child-titled account, LP-034 educational floor. The father's month of retaliatory access-denial is coercion against exercise of a Charter right — in -2, per the Clean-Record Doctrine, it is *logged* while the community determines the response (thin enforcement is the layer's design, not a hole; the relocation itself still executes through the sovereign channel LP-044 protects). Her mother faces nothing; parental surrender and prenatal election channels would also have been open to her without STI notation.

**Seams exercised:** Art. VIII (right) → LP-068 (execution machinery) → Civic Floor Act (non-withdrawable channel) → Clean-Record Doctrine (coercion + organic-discovery hedge) → LP-023/034 (destination). Meshed cleanly; the corpus twice examined and deliberately declined mandatory notification of the right (LP-068's declined instruments), and the scenario respects that hedge — Nia's discovery was organic.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure what concrete consequence, if any, actually lands on the father inside -2 beyond the log entry, which the corpus deliberately leaves to the community.

### S6 [ORGANIC] — Phasing Out of Sanctuary
**Scenario:** Ideh Maros, 118, has lived in +1 Sanctuary for forty years and holds memberships in two Selective Ascension Domains — a contemplative-practice SAD and a research SAD gated on sustained publication metrics. Over three years she runs a side venture selling curated access to her Sanctuary social circle to Main Layer status-seekers — nothing criminal, but a pattern of transactional trust-trading that peers rate harshly. A high-impact trust violation entry (a broken fiduciary commitment in the venture) lands on her visible ledger; her STI drops from 91 to 83, below the 85-point floor. She protests that no judge ever heard her side and that losing her home, both SADs, and her standing over "one number" violates the Charter's own multi-factor guarantee.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article VII** — controlling. "Phasing governs movement between Main Layer (0) and +1 Sanctuary — it is STI-driven, reversible... A Sanctuary resident whose STI falls below the 85-point eligibility floor or who commits a high-impact trust violation phases back to Main Layer and must re-earn ascension through standard criteria."
2. **Articles XII & XIII** — the exemption she misreads: "Layer phasing... is exempt from this rule and may be triggered by a single-metric threshold, because phasing is a return to baseline rather than a punitive descent and the metric threshold is itself the definition of the layer's qualifying condition."
3. **Two-Level Moral Causality** (Founding Corpus) — "condition-based consequence is automatic adjustment when a continuous qualifying condition is no longer met and adjudicates nothing."
4. **The Domain Boundary Act** (Founding Corpus) — "A citizen who ceases to meet a criterion is automatically excluded, returned to Sanctuary or to Main Layer if the phasing condition has lapsed, with no criminal enforcement, no metric impact from the exclusion itself and full re-qualification eligibility if the metric is restored."
5. **Charter Article II** — 10:1 penalty-to-recovery ratio; layer-contextual public rating.
6. **The Record Contestation Act** (Founding Corpus) — external civil-court channel if she disputes the ledger entry's context.

**Ruling:** Ideh phases to Main. No judge is owed: phasing is condition-based, not punitive — Articles XII/XIII exempt it from multi-factor evaluation by design, and Two-Level Moral Causality classifies it as automatic adjustment that "adjudicates nothing." Her SAD memberships end by automatic exclusion with no additional penalty and full re-qualification eligibility — and because this is phasing, not punitive reassignment, the Domain Boundary Act's harsher rule (punitive descent "erases every domain membership so that the stack cannot be re-acquired") does not apply. Her ledger entry is contestable through the civil-court channel, but contestation "adjusts how the record is contextualized, not whether the event happened." Re-earning 85 under the 10:1 ratio is a years-scale project; Sanctuary is "not a reward to be collected, but a standard to be continuously met." On Main entry she is auto-enrolled in LP-062.2 sexual-assault TIP coverage like any layer entrant; her formerly mandatory Sanctuary implant posture becomes user-configurable.

**Seams exercised:** Art. VII (phasing) ↔ Arts. XII/XIII (single-metric exemption) ↔ Domain Boundary Act (exclusion vs. erasure fork) ↔ Two-Level Moral Causality (classification by mechanism). Meshed cleanly — the phasing/punitive distinction is carried consistently across all four instruments.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether the *high-impact trust violation* branch of Art. VII phases her independently of the score (it appears disjunctive: "or"), which would have phased her even at STI 86.

### S7 [ORGANIC] — Visiting the Brother Who Sealed the Ceiling
**Scenario:** Dario Enns, Main Layer, visits his brother Luca, who filed voluntary permanent residency in a -3 voluntary cooperative district six years ago after psychological screening and the formal upward-pathway waiver. At the gate complex Dario signs the documented vessel-link suspension, rents an implant-paired defensive sidearm, and transfers $10,000 of Main currency for spending money. He spends nine days in the district — casinos, an unregulated AR-overlay bazaar, a Colosseum arena he watches but does not enter. On day six he develops a serious infection; the district's private clinic treats him for a punishing fee in freedom tokens. He returns on schedule: weapon relinquished (it was Main-class, so he re-carries it home), link restored at exit, leftover tokens left with Luca. He asks the gate officer when Luca can visit *him*.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-004.2, Backup Vessel Parity — Second Pass** (Federal, 2115) — controlling for the boundary: "In -3 Terminal, backup vessel coverage does not cross the boundary: entry requires documented vessel-link suspension, severing the link for the duration of the visit and restoring it on exit. Death inside -3 is final for visitor and resident alike."
2. **Charter III.V (Downward Visitation) + Status-Based vs. Territorial Jurisdiction** (Founding Corpus) — visitor retains origin status, assets, thresholds; "the visitor's implant ledger records any act regardless of location."
3. **LP-049, Cross-Layer Weapon Transit Specification** — origin-layer authorization governs; rental weapons implant-paired; lower-layer classes relinquished at departure.
4. **The Central Banking Authority** — downward transfer at the retention schedule, ~3,100 freedom tokens per $10,000 at midpoint (Charter III.IV illustration); tokens are -3-siloed.
5. **Charter Article XXIV** — "The private market determines healthcare access... This is not a failure of the civilization's technology. It is the consequence of the Freedom Layer's own logic."
6. **Charter Article VII** — "Punitive residents hold no visitation rights to higher layers. Voluntary permanent residents of -3 retain no upward visitation rights."

**Ruling:** Every element is governed. The suspension is an infrastructure-integrity rule, not risk-transfer — for nine days Dario was mortal, and the infection was his own private-market problem exactly as Article XXIV prices it: no guaranteed medical access in -3, drone response ends at the boundary. His conduct was evaluated against Main thresholds throughout (status-based jurisdiction); watching the Colosseum is not an act. The weapon mechanics ran precisely per LP-049's three rules. The leftover tokens lawfully stay in -3 — the silo admits no upward path. The answer to his final question is *never*: Luca's filing permanently closed the upward pathway including visitation; the relationship continues only by Dario going down. (Had Dario been engaged in commercial activity rather than visiting family, LP-006 would additionally have required public disclosure of home-layer status and vessel-coverage terms.)

**Seams exercised:** LP-004.2 (boundary hardware) → LP-049 (weapon supply-side) → Central Banking (settlement) → Art. XXIV (medical gradient) → Art. VII (one-way visitation). Five instruments, no daylight between them.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure of the precise re-entry processing burden (Article X's "moral accounting" applies to exit/re-entry from the civilization, not inter-layer return; inter-layer return transit is standard, but the corpus never itemizes the returning visitor's gate procedure beyond link restoration).

### S8 [ORGANIC] — The Storefront That Stopped Clearing
**Scenario:** Henrick Om, Main Layer, owns his primary residence, a vacation flat his adult daughter occupies as her primary home, and a commercial storefront he inherited in 2303. The storefront's tenant left in early 2305; Henrick, busy and sentimental, leaves it empty. In mid-2306, thirteen months later, his monthly SCM statement suddenly jumps: the storefront's assessed value of $900,000 now attributes into his savings balance, and his district's 90-day rolling average has been above the $100B trigger all year, so the 10% monthly pulse is biting the full attributed amount. His accountant proposes a fix: a friend's shell company will "lease" the storefront and route a token settlement through the central bank each quarter to reset the idle window.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-069, Savings Attribution and Property-Cap Repeal Act** (Federal, 2220) — controlling. Commercial idleness: "property that clears no central-bank settlements for a sustained window (12 consecutive months...) attributes at assessed value until settlement resumes or the asset sells, with the clearing record as the sole sensor." Residential: primary residence unconditionally exempt; a residence another household occupies as their primary home "reads as working capital and does not attribute."
2. **LP-070, Idle-Commercial Enforcement Act** (Federal, 2211) — the trap on the fix: "a cleared settlement is a representation of genuine economic activity; a settlement staged to simulate activity is evasion under the system-sabotage classification, and an adjudicated finding runs liability retroactive to the first simulated reset."
3. **Charter III.VII** — pulse-at-start principle; 10%/month at the $100B district-aggregate trigger (LP-070 parameters); no floor, no exemptions, no means testing.
4. **The Settlement-Layer Collection Act** (Founding Corpus) — "money laundering and tax evasion are treated as system sabotage with maximum enforcement priority."

**Ruling:** The attribution is correct: primary residence exempt, daughter's flat exempt as another household's primary home, storefront attributed in full after twelve clear-less months — the clearing record is the sole sensor and no inspector ever visited. Henrick's lawful exits are exactly two: resume genuine settlement (real tenant, real activity) or sell. The accountant's fix is the precise conduct LP-070 names: a staged settlement simulating activity is evasion under the system-sabotage classification — reassignable, with liability retroactive to the first simulated reset, recovering every avoided pulse. The continuous pattern scan on the clearing record (minimal settlement frequency against assessed value) is built to flag exactly a quarterly token payment on a $900,000 asset. Both Henrick and the shell-company friend would carry it individually (Individual Attribution Act).

**Seams exercised:** LP-069 (attribution base) → LP-070 (enforcement + staged-settlement classification) → III.VII (pulse mechanics) → Settlement-Layer Collection (sabotage classification). Meshed cleanly — LP-070 was drafted onto LP-069's base by design and the seam shows no gap.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether a *below-market but genuine* lease (real tenant, real occupancy, nominal rent) would clear the "genuine economic activity" test; the statute's sensor is clearing frequency, not price adequacy, and a genuinely occupied storefront at low rent appears lawful.

### S9 [ORGANIC] — The Widow and the Compliance Tokens
**Scenario:** Sela and Bram Okonjo married in Main Layer in 2290 under the LP-025 regime. In 2298 Bram filed voluntary permanent residency in -1 — the marriage survived his descent; Sela stayed in Main and visited twice a year. Bram prospered modestly in the -1 economy: a repair cooperative share and a compliance-token balance worth years of local living. In 2306 Bram dies in a workshop fire. The -1 fabrication proxy attempts revival; it fails — the 1-in-10,000 event. The failure is permanent. Bram's -1 cooperative initially tells Sela the estate is "a -1 matter for -1 residents." Sela asserts heirship and asks whether she can bring the estate's value home to Main.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-079, Cross-Layer Marriage Carry Act** (Federal, 2304) — controlling. "A marriage validly formed under any layer's family-law regime keeps its incidents — heirship, spousal property attribution, medical and incapacity consent... through elective residency, voluntary permanent residency, punitive reassignment, and visitation... it bars only the evaporation of a recognized marriage at a gate."
2. **Charter III.IV via LP-079's currency-wall clause** — "heirship recognition is not value transit... an upper-layer heir's claim on a lower-denominated estate confers standing, never upward carriage."
3. **The Continuity Integrity Act** (Founding Corpus) — "a failed revival is permanent, offering no template-based second attempt"; the Continuity Reliability Schedule publishes -1 at ~1-in-10,000.
4. **The Institutional Archive Act** (Founding Corpus) — active-status flag transitions to deceased; upper-layer family may view the public record; "cannot revive, contact or reconstitute the deceased."
5. **LP-025** — the Main+Sanctuary implementation, continuing unamended beneath LP-079.

**Ruling:** The cooperative is wrong: since 2304 the marriage's incidents carry across every boundary the spouses' statuses span, and -1 is an institutionally present layer where the consent-and-recognition interface binds outright (the merely-advisory posture applies only to private -3 institutions). Sela inherits. What she inherits, however, lives and dies in -1: her claim confers standing over the tokens and the cooperative share, never upward carriage — she may hold, direct, spend, or gift the value within the -1 economy (including during visits), but no unit of it converts to Main currency, ever. The revival failure is permanent under the binary semantics; no second attempt exists at any price. The register itself records that this exact tension — "heirship without upward carriage is recognition in name," argued by a Main minority — was raised and answered at ratification: Article III.IV admits no exception.

**Seams exercised:** LP-025 → LP-079 (inter-tier carry a layer-wide instrument couldn't enact) → III.IV silo (the wall LP-079 deliberately left standing) → Continuity Integrity (revival failure) → Archive Act (post-death record). Meshed cleanly; the one rough edge is by-design.

**Verdict:** COVERED-WITH-FRICTION — the friction is real but *ratified*: an heir with standing over value she can never repatriate holds a genuinely awkward asset, and the corpus's answer (use it down there or let it sit) was contested on the record and deliberately kept. This is a named cost, not a gap.
**Confidence:** high — least sure of the mechanics by which Sela administers -1 property from Main between visits (agency/custodianship inside -1 is left to the layer's private order).

### S10 [ORGANIC] — The Man Who Drinks in Public
**Scenario:** Tomas Reyes, 47, Main Layer, drinks hard and often. He harms no one — no fights, no impaired driving, no dependents neglected. But four times in 2305 he is found passed out in a transit plaza outside the designated venues, and each time collects the LP-018 ticketed fine for public impairment. His visible ledger now shows a sustained impairment pattern; acquaintances rate him poorly and his STI has sagged. A neighbor, irritated, files a petition comment demanding Tomas be "sent down to -1 where that behavior belongs." Tomas, sobering up, pays his fines, completes a voluntary treatment program, and asks whether he is on a countdown to reassignment.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article XXVI** — controlling. "A citizen who uses substances and causes no harm to others incurs no criminal liability and no layer consequence... Chronic self-harm without third-party harm is an STI matter... No criminal enforcement. No punitive reassignment."
2. **LP-018, Public Intoxication** (Layer-Wide, Main) — "Restricted to designated venues; ticketed fines for public impairment."
3. **Charter Article XV** — "Minor infractions within Main Layer are clearable and amendable... The threshold for -1 reassignment is not a countdown. It is an evaluation of trajectory. Correction resets the trajectory."
4. **Charter Article I** — the pattern pathway requires "sustained accumulation of minor infractions *without remediation*"; "A resident who accumulates and corrects is demonstrating exactly the behavioral trajectory the system is designed to recognize."
