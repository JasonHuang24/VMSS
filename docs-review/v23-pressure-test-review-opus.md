## Defects found (worst first)

**D1 — S5 Dren sub-ruling: refuted.** Opus ruled that Dren, a -2 resident restraining an attacker in his own layer, has no statutory shield and falls back to bare Article XIV — and listed "resident-defender asymmetry" as ranked finding #5. LP-048.3's actual text (law-polling.html:1821–1856) carries no visitor gate: "relationship-agnostic," protection for "any witness," and its foreign-national clause contrasts against a default "citizen-defender." The more faithful reading is that Dren is an LP-048.3 beneficiary in his own right. Opus's own confidence note admitted the ambiguity its ruling asserted as settled. **Ranked finding #5 is withdrawn**; at most a one-line scope clarification to LP-048.3 remains as an optional tidy-up. The Mara holding stands untouched.

**D2 — S17 dual-key scope stretch.** Charter XXVIII.III's dual-key classification is textually only a regulatory-vs-structural screen; Opus used it as a general tier-conflict kill mechanism, bridging via LP-062.3's different taxonomy ("constitutional conflict, not calibration"). The substantive outcome (petition dies on tier hierarchy + Implant Instrumentation Act's closed consequence set) is right; the disposition mechanics are synthesis, not settled procedure.

**D3 — Misattributions (real text, wrong instrument; outcomes unaffected):**

## The S20 gap — confirmed, with one precondition

The whole-canon sweep found **no operative instrument anywhere** governing AGI self-instantiation as person-creation. Whitepaper §22.7–22.10 and the FAQ cover AGI rights, embodiment, and continuity — never origination. All nine of Opus's envelope citations are verbatim-accurate. Three adjacent finds:

1. **Iara Voss, "Sanctuary Consensus, Year 626"** (simulations.html:3292–3369): the Long Horizon simulation's proposed Article XXXIV "Distributed Citizenship" — one identity across two bodies, on its seventh failed attempt across ~600 years, opponents arguing the amendment "forks" continuity. Mirror-image of the Meridian case (shared identity vs. claimed new person). Corroborates the gap; doesn't close it.
2. **⚠ Valdirenze tension** (simulations.html:3265–3281): Ren Oshiro's four private virtual worlds hold ~16,000 AGI residents described as holding "full VMSS personhood" with STI scores — depicted as normal, with no admission process, no fiscal accounting, no adjudication. Either those residents are not UBI-drawing citizens despite the personhood language (an unaddressed ambiguity), or wider canon already normalizes exactly the mass person-creation exposure S20 raises. **This needs an authorial ruling before LP-077 drafting** — it determines whether the statute regularizes an existing practice or governs a first instance, and it undercuts the scenario's "no VMSS citizen has done this" framing as literally stated.
3. Whitepaper failed-revival doctrine (whitepaper.html:1059): a template-grown duplicate body is "a separate person with the same DNA and none of the continuity," destroyed to foreclose duplicate-identity claims — supports treating a fork as a distinct person, and shows canon already destroys accidental duplicates rather than admitting them.
