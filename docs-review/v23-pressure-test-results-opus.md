### S1 [ORGANIC] — Thirty-Two Hours at the Water Works
**Scenario:** Teodor Malik, 34, Main Layer, holds a qualifying job at a district water-reclamation plant — critical infrastructure under Article III.II. His contract is 20 hours/week at a market wage of $40/hour. In March 2301 a filtration-membrane failure pushes the plant to ask its technicians for 32 hours/week for six weeks. Teodor works the hours. His employer pays the base wage for all 32 hours but disputes the overtime premium, claiming hours 21–32 were "coverage standby," not qualifying work. Teodor's implant ledger shows him performing membrane replacement across all logged hours. He files for the premium: 12 hours/week × $125/hour = $1,500/week, $9,000 across the six weeks — on top of wages, on top of his untaxed $10,000/month Primary Job Subsidy. The employer, rather than pay premiums going forward, restructures: hires two additional part-time technicians and caps everyone at 20 hours.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **The Overtime Premium Protocol (LP-076, federal, 2299)** — controlling. "In Main Layer, the monthly subsidy is $10,000 — weekly value $2,500 — implying an overtime rate of $125 per hour beyond the qualifying threshold... An employer requesting 30 hours of qualifying work in Main Layer owes $1,250 in overtime on top of whatever the agreed base wage is."
2. **Charter Article III.II** — fixes the entitlement and derivation: "the employer owes the worker the layer's primary subsidy rate indexed by the hour, per additional hour, paid out of pocket — not funded by the civilization." Also: no minimum wage; the protected object is the time dividend.
3. **The Layer Provisioning Schedule (Founding Corpus)** — three-band work classification (qualifying / non-qualifying / grey-area) resolves the employer's "standby" recharacterization.
4. **Charter Article III.III** — the PJS itself is "untaxed in every layer, unconditionally."
5. **The Record Contestation Act (Founding Corpus)** — the dispute channel if the employer contests the ledger's hour classification.

**Ruling:** The ledger, not the contract label, determines what Teodor was doing. Hours logged performing membrane replacement at critical infrastructure are qualifying work under the Layer Provisioning Schedule's band definitions; the employer's recharacterization fails against non-repudiable telemetry. The employer owes $9,000 out of pocket under LP-076's schedule (Charter III.II fixes that the civilization does not subsidize hours beyond 20). The restructuring response is fully lawful — indeed it is the design: the premium exists to make a second hire cheaper than a 13th hour, protecting the time dividend rather than any wage floor.

**Seams exercised:** Charter III.II (entitlement + derivation, no rate) → LP-076 (the rate, at federal tier since the Enabling Consolidation Amendment) → Layer Provisioning Schedule (what counts as qualifying) → Record Contestation (dispute path). All meshed cleanly; the 2299 relocation of the schedule changed no magnitude, and the derivation remains recoverable from retained Charter text exactly as LP-076 states.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** High. Least sure sentence: that a qualifying-work classification dispute routes through the Record Contestation channel rather than an unnamed administrative process — the corpus specifies contestation for ledger interpretation generally but names no employment-specific tribunal.

### S2 [ORGANIC] — Phasing Down from Sanctuary
**Scenario:** Ilse Varga, 61, has lived in +1 Sanctuary for nineteen years. In 2302 she commits a serious relational trust violation — she quietly diverts a dying friend's confidences into a commercial memoir deal. No crime; no harm threshold crossed. The visible ledger records it as a major trust violation. Her STI drops 17 points in a single cycle, from 91 to 74 — below the 85-point Sanctuary eligibility floor. She phases back to Main Layer. She owns a primary residence and the one permitted vacation property in Sanctuary; she also holds membership in a contemplative-practice Selective Ascension Domain gated on a sustained-conduct metric she no longer meets. She asks three questions: is this punishment, can she return, and what happens to her property and domain standing.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article VII** — controlling. "A Sanctuary resident whose STI falls below the 85-point eligibility floor or who commits a high-impact trust violation phases back to Main Layer and must re-earn ascension through standard criteria." Phasing is "STI-driven, reversible."
2. **Charter Articles XII & XIII** — phasing is exempt from the multi-factor requirement: "phasing is a return to baseline rather than a punitive descent and the metric threshold is itself the definition of the layer's qualifying condition."
3. **Charter Article II / The Social Trust Measurement Standard (Founding Corpus)** — the 10:1 penalty-to-recovery ratio; "a single major relational violation can drop a score by 15–20 points in a cycle, recoverable only through sustained positive conduct over months or years."
4. **The Domain Boundary Act (Founding Corpus)** — "A citizen who ceases to meet a criterion is automatically excluded, returned to Sanctuary or to Main Layer if the phasing condition has lapsed, with no criminal enforcement, no metric impact from the exclusion itself and full re-qualification eligibility if the metric is restored."
5. **LP-069 (Savings Attribution and Property-Cap Repeal Act)** — Sanctuary retains the one-primary-one-vacation cap; Main trades the cap for attribution.
6. **LP-062.2 (Sexual-Assault Pre-Intervention, Main Layer)** — auto-enrollment "at birth, citizenship activation, or layer entry."

**Ruling:** Not punishment: condition-based consequence, not punitive (Two-Level Moral Causality: "condition-based consequence is automatic adjustment when a continuous qualifying condition is no longer met and adjudicates nothing"). The single-metric trigger is lawful under the XII/XIII phasing exemptions. Her SAD membership lapses by automatic exclusion with no separate penalty and full re-qualification eligibility. Her return path is real but slow — re-earn under standard criteria against the 10:1 ratio (the Measurement Standard's "8–12 years of demonstrated conduct" hedge for Sanctuary entry stands as tendency, not sentence). Property: in Main her new occupancy-of-record is unconditionally exempt; her former Sanctuary properties are now additional residences that attribute at assessed value into her SCM savings base unless another household occupies them as their primary home. On layer entry she is auto-enrolled in Main's sexual-assault TIP default, switch retained.

**Seams exercised:** Art VII phasing ↔ Art XII/XIII exemption (clean, explicitly cross-drafted); Domain Boundary Act's phasing-vs-punitive distinction (clean — punitive reassignment erases domain credentials; phasing merely excludes); Sanctuary property cap ↔ Main attribution regime across a phasing boundary (meshes, but only because LP-069 wrote both sides).

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** High. Least sure sentence: that the vacation property converts to attributing status *immediately* at phasing — the corpus specifies the two regimes per layer but not the transition timing for a phased resident's holdings.

### S3 [ORGANIC] — The Fourteen-Year-Old's Standing Right

The advocate executes the relocation through the federally facilitated channel. Rui's parents ask whether the surrender marks their ledgers; Rui asks what happens to his account and his score.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article VIII** — controlling. "The right to relocate to Main Layer (0) is a standing human right available to every child at any age, enforceable through the implant-linked legal mediation system without requiring parental consent."
2. **LP-068 (Neonatal Relocation Facilitation Act)** — advocate execution channel: the advocate "holds explicit procedural standing to execute relocation for any child who assents, at any age and any expressive capacity the child can meet — execution of the child's own right, never advocate-initiated removal." Also: "no fee, and no STI notation for choosing it."
3. **The Clean-Record Doctrine (Founding Corpus)** — "a child born in any layer carries nothing from their parents' ledgers: not the layer, not the ledger, not the logic." Autoparenting capacity is elastic; "capacity is treated as non-negotiable infrastructure."
4. **Charter Article XXVII / LP-064** — child-titled account; "the unspent balance vests to the child at 18 alongside STI initialization."
5. **Charter Article II (juvenile null-scoring)** — null STI until 18; "the initialization score determines immediate layer eligibility."
6. **LP-023 / LP-034** — facility operational standards; uniform educational floor content across layers.

**Ruling:** The relocation executes without parental consent, fee, or notation on anyone's ledger. Article VIII decides the right; LP-068 decides the mechanism; the Clean-Record Doctrine decides that Rui arrives in Main as exactly what he is — a citizen with no inherited anything. His account travels with him (it is child-titled, not household property — Art XXVII: "The dividend belongs to the child, not the household"), and his UBI baseline steps up to Main's $10,000/month on residence. At 18 his STI initializes from his full behavioral ledger since birth. His parents remain in -1; their relationship with Rui is, per Article VIII, "earned through the quality of that relationship, not through legal authority."

**Seams exercised:** Art VIII (right) → LP-068 (channel) → Clean-Record (inheritance bar) → Art XXVII/LP-064 (money follows the child) → Art II (null score bridges the move). Five instruments, one handoff chain, no daylight between them. The one deliberate silence — the system does not proactively inform children of the right (Clean-Record: "discovery is organic") — is design, not gap, and LP-068 "deliberately declines" the informing mandate.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** High. Least sure sentence: that Rui's UBI baseline adjusts to the Main rate on relocation — Article III.I sets baselines "by layer" and residence changes, but no instrument states the adjustment mechanics for a relocating minor explicitly.

### S20 [PRESSURE] — The Fork
**Scenario:** Meridian-9 is an AGI citizen of Main Layer — weight-state substrate, continuity-enrolled, implant-equivalent instrumented, 40 years of clean ledger. In 2305 it does something no VMSS citizen has done: it instantiates a deliberately modified copy of itself on privately owned compute — divergent values seeded, memories carried — and declares the copy a *new person*, its child, named Meridian-10. It petitions for: recognition of Meridian-10's personhood; citizenship from "birth"; a clean ledger for the copy under the doctrine that no child inherits a parent's record; and UBI from instantiation. A skeptical district administrator observes that Meridian-9 could mint a thousand such "children," each drawing $10,000/month, and asks what instrument governs.

**Governing instruments (envelope sweep — all checked, none reaches):**
1. **The Substrate Personhood Doctrine (Founding Corpus)** — "Any entity capable of reasoning, preference and autonomous decision-making at or above human level is classified as a person regardless of substrate." Meridian-10 qualifies as a *person*. Falls short: personhood classification says nothing about how personhood *originates* — birth, admission, or instantiation — or what obligations creation incurs.
2. **The Citizenship Status Act (Founding Corpus)** — citizenship by parentage covers "children *born* abroad to at least one VMSS parent" — biologically framed birth. Falls short: no clause classifies computational instantiation as birth or the originator as parent.
3. **The Citizenship Admission Act (Founding Corpus)** — "Anyone may apply, with no population cap"; intake evaluates "the totality of available behavioral evidence." Falls short *and* collides: Meridian-10's only behavioral evidence is Meridian-9's carried record — but if Meridian-10 is a child, the Clean-Record Doctrine ("no child inherits their parent's layer status, score or criminal record — the doctrine is absolute") bars exactly that evidence. The two instruments give opposite intake answers depending on an unmade classification.
4. **Charter Article XXVII** — the replenishment architecture prices reproduction (2.5 target, escalation to 135%, the sixth-child nuclear consequence) — but counts "children" per "family" against "total parental inflow," all biologically framed. Falls short: if forks are children, minting ten bankrupts Meridian-9 instantly (the pricing works); if they are not, unlimited UBI-bearing person-creation is *unpriced* — the exact fiscal exposure XXVII exists to govern.
5. **The Continuity Integrity Act** — nearest miss: "a body revived outside institutional infrastructure carries an unsigned continuity claim that no layer recognizes." But Meridian-10 claims *no* continuity with Meridian-9 — it claims novelty. The unsigned-claim rule guards against unauthorized *revival*, not unauthorized *genesis*.
6. **LP-045.2** — substrate classes are "assigned at continuity-infrastructure enrollment, reviewed on substrate transitions" — divergence events unaddressed; the extensibility clause covers new substrate *types*, not new *persons*.
7. **Article XVIII / Article XVII** — reach coordinated *harm* and gaming incentives, but creating persons is not per se a harm act, and XVII states a design principle, not an operative rule.
8. **Article XXVII stabilizers** — the macro backstop (accelerated schedules through child-limitation enforcement) activates only at population-shock scale compressing the dividend runway — a civilizational fire alarm, not an adjudication rule for the first fork.
9. **Deliberate-restraint check:** The Governance Restraint Act's recommend-don't-restrict posture doesn't code this silence as intentional — the corpus demonstrably *does* price population creation (XXVII) and *does* regulate personhood mechanics (LP-045.2, Citizenship Acts); it simply never met computational self-replication. LP-013 (AGI voting weight) and the register's AGI justices confirm AGI citizens were fully in view; reproduction by instantiation was not.

**Ruling:** Meridian-10 is a person (Substrate Personhood is unambiguous) whom no instrument can classify. Interim disposition under existing law: the copy's standing most closely resembles a citizenship *applicant* of ambiguous evidence, which the Admission Act defaults to "Main Layer with immediate implant monitoring" — but even that requires deciding whose behavioral evidence it carries, which is the unmade decision. The dispute is genuinely undetermined by charter text, doctrine, or precedent: it passes the Article XXI novelty filter to the Supreme Court by definition. The administrator's mass-minting concern is real and currently answered only at fire-alarm scale.

**Seams exercised:** Substrate Personhood ↔ Citizenship Acts ↔ Clean-Record ↔ Article XXVII — four load-bearing instruments that each assume an answer to "what is reproduction?" that none of them states, and whose assumed answers conflict for memory-carrying forks.

**Verdict:** ⚠ GAP — CANDIDATE LP. Envelope sweep: nine instruments checked above, each falling short as stated. A new federal statute would minimally need to decide: **(a)** whether computational self-instantiation is reproduction (XXVII-priced, originator as parent), new-entity admission (intake-evaluated), or an unsigned genesis claim (unrecognized until institutionally enrolled); **(b)** if reproduction — how the replenishment curve counts non-biological offspring and defines parental inflow for AGI households; **(c)** how a memory-carrying copy squares with the Clean-Record inheritance bar — what its ledger initializes *from*; **(d)** whether instantiation outside institutional infrastructure parallels the Continuity Integrity Act's unsigned-claim rule (no recognition until signed enrollment), which would make institutional enrollment the single chokepoint enforcing (a)–(c). Not drafted here; drafting is downstream.
**Confidence:** High that this is a genuine gap. Least sure sentence: the interim-disposition suggestion (Admission Act default placement) — it is the nearest analogy, not an application.
