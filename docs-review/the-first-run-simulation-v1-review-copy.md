## I. Constitution

It took the chambers seven months to seat the Commission, and the
difficulty was the law working as written. The historical quarantine
had removed from eligibility nearly every entity whose name a fiscal
estimator would recognize — the authors, advocates, opponents, and
adjudicators of two generations of TAX-50 proceedings — and the
Meritboard's rankings, consulted under §1.1, kept returning entities
whose distinction lay in adjacent fields. The Register had predicted
this in its eighth engraving and called it the promise, not the
defect.

The three who were seated: **Ilex Varn**, human, a fiscal estimator
whose career had run through the obligations accounting of the outer
sanctuaries; **Cadastre**, a substrate-native causal-inference entity
whose published work concerned attribution in traced-flow systems and
who had never once written about taxation; and **Odal Ferre**, human,
an audit methodologist old enough to remember when the SCM ledger was
an argument instead of an infrastructure. Between them they covered
the three competences of §1.2 — barely, the commentators said, and
the commentators were not wrong, and it did not matter, because the
Charter had been built so that it would not matter.

The panels seated faster. The certification-side panel arrived with
its theory already famous — the velocity case: that retained capital
in the upper pair compresses time, that the discretionary economy's
products arrive years before the public allocation function would
have reached them, that under a discount rate the years are the
value. The refusal-side panel arrived with the older theory: that in
a post-scarcity stack the binding constraint is talent and attention,
not capital; that private deployment reshuffles ownership of
ventures that were coming anyway; that the displacement netting of
A.1.4, honestly computed, eats the margin whole.

Neither panel argued. The Charter had abolished the venue for it.
They added.

## II. The design fight

The fight, when it came, was conducted entirely in specification.

The certification side built for precision. Their Finding III
projection — the concentration question, activation frequency under
the 50 schedule — used the ledger's trailing decade (~ mean 41
activation events per year, ~ dispersion 9) and a tight structural
model family whose thirty-year worst-year bound they could hold, they
believed, within the Charter's tolerance.

The refusal side did its duty. Under §4.4 it added three concentration
models of its own — each surviving the §4.3 floor, each beating the
persistence benchmark out-of-sample, each carrying wider projection
uncertainty than the certification side's, and none excludable,
because the floor was the only gate and the floor was mechanical.
Cadastre, who as a Commission seat could exclude nothing either, was
heard to remark that the union was performing exactly as designed,
and that this was the problem.

Then the arithmetic arrived. §5.3's ceiling is a formula: the
controlling interval for Finding III may be no wider than the
distance between the threshold and the baseline mean — 125 percent
of ~41 is ~54; the distance is ~13 events. The simultaneous one-sided
95 percent worst-year bound over thirty projected years, taken across
the full union — the certification side's tight family and the
refusal side's lawful additions together — computed to a width of
~34 events.

Thirty-four, against a ceiling of thirteen.

There was no argument to have. The formula is arithmetic; the
Registrar computes it. The design could not decide Finding III in
either direction — could not certify, could not fail, could only
guess — and the Charter had been built, at the cost of sixty-two
findings and a presidential veto held in reserve, so that guessing
was the one thing the instrument would never do.

## III. The lock that never came

The Commission filed anyway. Some said desperation, some said
diligence: filing put the question to the checklist, and the
checklist's answer would publish either way.

The Registrar's determination under §9.2 ran four pages and turned on
one sentence: *the stated precision cannot decide the Finding's
threshold in either direction, and a design that cannot decide is not
a test and may not be locked.* Attached, per the Charter's habit of
showing its work: the width computation, the ceiling computation, and
the union membership over which both had been taken.

The certification-side panel appealed — the first §2.4 appeal in the
instrument's life. Their theory was elegant: the width ceiling, they
argued, is methodological judgment wearing arithmetic's clothes, and
judgment belongs to no Registrar; the determination was merits, and
merits determinations are void on their face. The Supreme Court took
nineteen days and disposed of it in a paragraph that will be cited
for a century: *the fence of §2.3 runs between deciding what to
measure and verifying what was measured; a subtraction is on the
verifying side; the Registrar computed a formula the chambers
adopted, and a formula adopted is a merit already decided.*

The determination stood. The design was never locked. In 2281, two
years from constitution with no lock achieved, the Commission
dissolved under §12.5 — without window consequence, without a void,
without a result. The window stands open. Nothing was consumed,
because nothing was tried; the Charter does not charge a civilization
for declining to guess.

## IV. What published anyway

No run means no record — but determinations publish, and the
Registrar's rejection became the most-read audit document of the
decade, because its appendix was, in effect, a bill of particulars
for the future:

- The ledger's concentration baseline is ten years deep. The width
  of a thirty-year worst-year bound falls with baseline depth; the
  appendix computed (~) that a twenty-five-year baseline, at current
  dispersion, brings the union's width inside the ceiling with room
  to spare.
- Ledger coverage — the ninth engraving's residue — stood at ~91
  percent of upper-stack flows. Every untraced flow widens the
  attribution intervals of Findings II and IV, and widening runs
  against activation, always.
- The refusal panel's widest model owed most of its width to the
  demographic-regime term. The canonical statistical authority's
  series on that term improves on its own schedule, indifferent to
  anyone's tax theory.

Read plainly: the appendix said *the showing may be possible, and it
is not possible yet, and here is the arithmetic of when.* The
velocity theory was not defeated. The displacement theory was not
vindicated. Finding IV — the old question, whether retained wealth
in a world past scarcity can still buy anything the world would
miss — was never computed at all. It waits, undecided, exactly as
open as it was the day the override was withdrawn, because the
instrument's first official act was to refuse to answer it with
insufficient evidence.

## V. The state of the law

2281, year-end. The schedule is seventy, thirty-five, seventeen,
eight, under LP-073, as it has been since the First Ratchet. The
window is open through 2288. A second Commission may be constituted
any day the chambers choose, and the arithmetic of the appendix says
the choice will get easier every year the ledger deepens.

In the sanctuary levels they tell it as a joke with a straight face:
the first thing the great tax instrument ever did was decline to be
used. The auditors tell it differently: the first thing it did was
hold, under the full weight of a motivated certifier, a dutiful
opposition, a formula, a fence, and an appeal — and holding is the
oldest promise in the register.

Rates fall when shown. Nothing was shown. Nothing fell. The
civilization is still building the light.
