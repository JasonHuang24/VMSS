### S1 [ORGANIC] — The Vacant Flat
**Scenario:** Doran Kessig, 52, Main Layer, owns his primary residence (occupancy of record on his implant ledger) plus a second unit he inherited three years ago. He never rented it out or moved anyone in — it sits furnished and empty, a hedge he keeps meaning to sell. His district's rolling 90-day savings average crosses the $100B activation threshold in March 2306 and the garnishing cycle activates. Doran's accountant tells him his *primary* residence is safe but asks pointed questions about the second unit's occupancy status, because a cousin has been "sort of living there on and off" for two months while between leases. Doran isn't sure whether that counts, and wants to know before the next pulse.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article III.VII** — controlling. "[A] citizen's primary residence, occupancy of record on the implant ledger, is unconditionally exempt; every additional residence attributes into the savings balance at assessed value and is garnished on the ordinary pulse whenever the district is active, except where another household occupies it as their own primary home, which counts as working capital and does not attribute. Vacant additional residences attribute in full."
2. **LP-069, Savings Attribution and Property-Cap Repeal Act** — federal specification: "the savings balance the mandate reads follows stored value wherever it is held, not the form it takes... Main Layer trades the cap for attribution."
3. **LP-070, Idle-Commercial Enforcement and Main-Treasury Architecture Act** — "the pulse-at-start principle the Charter retains reads against that rate: a citizen holding $100,000 at the opening of a monthly cycle owes 10% on that amount regardless of spending, transfers, or drawdowns."
4. **Article III.VII, pulse-at-start principle** — obligation locks at the balance held at the cycle's opening.

**Ruling:** Doran's primary residence is unconditionally exempt regardless of district activation. The second unit is the live question, and it turns on a factual determination the Charter itself supplies the test for, not a gap: does the cousin occupy it "as their own primary home"? Two months of intermittent occupancy while between leases reads as transient use, not primary-home occupancy — the implant ledger's own occupancy-of-record mechanism (the same one that establishes Doran's primary residence) is the evidentiary standard, and a cousin who has not re-registered that address as their own occupancy of record has not converted it to working capital. Absent that registration, the unit attributes at assessed value on the ordinary pulse. If the cousin's stay solidifies into genuine primary occupancy and gets registered, attribution stops going forward — but retroactive relief for the intervening cycles is not available; the pulse-at-start principle locks each cycle's obligation independently, with no correction for a status that changes mid-cycle.

**Seams exercised:** Charter III.VII (residence exemption + attribution rule) → LP-069 (form-agnostic savings base) → LP-070 (pulse mechanics, exact rate/threshold). Meshed cleanly — the "own primary home" test resolves the ambiguity Doran's accountant raised without needing to reach for anything outside the text.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether the implant ledger's occupancy-of-record update is instantaneous or carries an administrative lag that would let a fast re-registration retroactively rescue a borderline cycle; no instrument states the registration's effective-date rule.

### S2 [ORGANIC] — Living on the Dividend, Anyway
**Scenario:** Sela and Boro Antuin, Main Layer, both quit market-rate jobs two years ago to care full-time for their two children, living on $20,000/month combined UBI plus Boro's single qualifying Primary Job Subsidy ($10,000/month) — $30,000/month total, no earned income. In 2306 their third child is born. They reason that since they have no earned income to tax, the replenishment escalation "has nothing to bite." Their civic account shows an assessment notice the following month regardless, and they file a contestation arguing the Act cannot reach money they never earned.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-064, Replenishment Assessment and Child Dividend Stewardship Act** — controlling. "The escalation percentages compute against total parental inflow: earned income, UBI, and Primary Job Subsidy combined." Third child: baseline 40% → 60%.
2. **Charter Article XXVII** — "every fiscal instrument applicable to the household is raised in concert, and the scheduled rate is measured against total parental inflow... Where the escalated instruments recover less than the scheduled rate, the shortfall is assessed directly against the parents as replenishment liability, so the curve binds identically on market earners and households living on the dividend."
3. **Charter Article III.III** — UBI/PJS untaxed at source, unconditionally, without exception.
4. **The Record Contestation Act** (Founding Corpus) — civil-court channel for disputed interpretation, not disputed law.

**Ruling:** The Antuins' contestation fails on the text they are contesting. Article XXVII states its own answer to precisely this argument: the curve "binds identically on market earners and households living on the dividend." Their measurement base is $30,000/month regardless of source; 60% of that is their scheduled liability. Because III.III's untaxed absolute is never breached — the $30,000 is never withheld or clawed back at source — the escalated instruments (which for a dividend-only household have no non-dividend income to draw from) recover $0, and the full $18,000/month scheduled amount accrues as replenishment liability rather than being collected in-kind. That liability compounds toward the Meritboard-set repayment deadline exactly as it would for an earner; not being reachable at source only changes the collection mechanism, not the existence of the debt. The contestation channel is available for a misread ledger entry, not for reinterpreting an Act whose own text names this exact scenario.

**Seams exercised:** Article XXVII (architecture) → LP-064 (measurement base explicitly including dividend income) → Article III.III (untaxed-at-source absolute, which the Antuins mistook for untaxed-in-substance) → Record Contestation Act (channel, correctly unavailable here). Meshed cleanly; the friction the Antuins found is the one the corpus deliberately closed.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure how long the Meritboard's repayment deadline runs before -1 reassignment triggers for a household with genuinely zero non-dividend inflow to satisfy it from, since no instrument states a floor case for a family with literally no earned-income lever to pull.

### S3 [ORGANIC] — Turning Eighteen
**Scenario:** Priya Oyelaran, Main Layer, turns 18 in 2306. Her implant ledger has recorded behavioral observations continuously since birth, but she has carried no STI score her entire life — the number simply did not exist. At 16 she had a minor altercation at school (resolved, no criminal flag); at 17 she volunteered extensively at a community clinic. Her parents, both high-STI professionals, assumed her score would initialize somewhere near theirs, "since she grew up around it." On her birthday the system computes her first score: 76. Her parents are surprised it isn't higher and ask whether population endorsement from their own high-standing social circle can push it up.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article II** — controlling. "At 18, the STI initializes: the system computes the citizen's first score using the full ledger of behavioral observations accumulated since birth, weighted by the standard trajectory formula. The initialization score determines immediate layer eligibility." Also: "Null is not a number. It is the absence of a score."
2. **The Social Trust Measurement Standard** (Founding Corpus) — "nobody initializes at 100 or at 0 but at a score reflecting eighteen years of observed behavior... new Main Layer entrants typically arrive in the 70–84 band."
3. **Same instrument** — "Population endorsement and disapproval accelerate or decelerate movement in the direction the behavioral data already indicates but cannot move a score against the grain of observed behavior."
4. **Charter Article II** — the 85-point Sanctuary eligibility floor "cannot evaluate a null input and therefore does not trigger" pre-18; it is live now.

**Ruling:** 76 is not a disappointing outlier — it is the expected band for a new adult entrant with an unremarkable, mixed record (a resolved minor incident, genuine volunteer service, nothing else notable). Her parents' assumption that proximity to high-STI adults would elevate her own score misreads the mechanism twice over: the formula is proprietary and weighted on her own eighteen years of observed conduct, not on household association, and the population-endorsement component the Standard describes explicitly cannot move a score "against the grain of observed behavior" — it can only accelerate movement the data already supports. Her parents' social circle vouching for her is not itself behavioral data. At 76 she is well below the 85-point Sanctuary floor and would need a sustained multi-year record to close that gap; nothing about her initialization is an error or a slight.

**Seams exercised:** Article II (null-scoring → initialization mechanic) → Social Trust Measurement Standard (initialization band, endorsement-cannot-override-data rule) → Article VII's 85-point floor (downstream consequence of the initialized number). Meshed cleanly; the parents' confusion is a real-world misunderstanding the text directly forecloses, not a doctrinal gap.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether the "standard trajectory formula" applied at initialization weights adolescent-era conduct (ages, say, 13–17) identically to conduct in the months immediately before 18, since the proprietary-formula provision deliberately withholds exactly this kind of internal weighting detail.

### S4 [ORGANIC] — The Pop-Up in -2
**Scenario:** Renn Askari, Main Layer entrepreneur, runs a two-week pop-up retail venture in a -2 commercial district, selling upper-layer fabricated goods to locals at a markup — legal downward commercial visitation. He advertises his stock as "Main Layer quality" but doesn't mention his own backup-vessel coverage or home-layer status anywhere in his signage or contracts, on the theory that it's his business, not his customers'. A local competitor files a complaint that Renn is trading on an implicit safety/quality halo without disclosing the very asymmetry (his continuity coverage, their lack of it in most transactions) that creates the halo.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-006, Lower-Layer Economic Disclosure Law** — controlling. "Requires upper-layer visitors engaged in commercial activity within lower layers to publicly disclose home-layer status and backup vessel coverage terms."
2. **Charter III.V, Downward Visitation** — Renn "retains their origin layer status, their origin layer assets, and their full institutional relationship with the civilization for the duration of the visit... remains subject to the enforcement posture of their origin layer while visiting."
3. **LP-004.2, Backup Vessel Parity — Second Pass** — disclosure "operates alongside that redraft's compromised-consent protections"; parity applies where "consent is structurally compromised... mass-commercial recruitment... under misleading representation of backup vessel coverage."
4. **Article XVIII, Network Attribution** (Charter anchor for LP-006) — the disclosure duty is anchored here because concealment of an upper-layer safety asymmetry inside a commercial relationship is the kind of context the corpus treats as legally load-bearing.

**Ruling:** Renn is in violation. LP-006 does not condition the disclosure duty on customer inquiry or on the transaction being high-risk — it requires public disclosure of home-layer status and vessel coverage terms as a standing condition of commercial activity by an upper-layer visitor in a lower layer, independent of what he chooses to advertise about his goods. His "it's my business, not theirs" framing gets the direction backward: the duty exists precisely because the asymmetry is invisible to -2 customers unless surfaced. This is not the LP-004.2 mortality-parity scenario (no hazardous-work recruitment is occurring here), so parity itself isn't in play, but the two instruments share the same underlying concern — commercial activity that trades on an undisclosed continuity asymmetry — and LP-006 is squarely the controlling one. Renn must post disclosure prominently; his origin-layer status and asset holdings are otherwise untouched (Downward Visitation), and the enforcement consequence for the disclosure failure itself is a civic-floor/regulatory matter local to the district, not a layer reassignment.

**Seams exercised:** Charter III.V (visitation status baseline) → LP-006 (disclosure duty, standing not contingent) → LP-004.2 (adjacent but distinct parity concern, correctly ruled inapplicable here). Meshed cleanly; the scenario tests whether an examiner would over-apply LP-004.2 by analogy, and the text keeps the two instruments separated by their actual triggering conditions.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** medium — least sure what specific enforcement mechanism polices an LP-006 disclosure failure (fine, STI flag, district-level sanction) since the statute states the duty but the consolidated code entry does not itself state a penalty schedule, leaving that to district-level regulatory implementation not shown in this corpus excerpt.

### S5 [ORGANIC] — A Death on a Tuesday
**Scenario:** Wen Xu, 71, Main Layer, collapses from cardiac arrest at home, alone, on a Tuesday morning. Her implant registers the event within seconds; medical drones arrive and confirm she is beyond field stabilization. No perpetrator, no crime — simple biological death. Her last scheduled sync completed six weeks earlier, well within the notification window. Her son, in +1 Sanctuary, wants to know exactly what happens next and whether his mother "comes back the same."

**Governing instruments:**
1. **The Enforcement Chain** (Founding Corpus) — controlling for sequence, though the perpetrator-processing branch is inert here: "the implant records the event with full contextual data, medical drones deploy to the victim and stabilize in the field, revival initiates if the victim dies and infrastructure is operational."
2. **The Medical Response Standard** (Founding Corpus) — "no insurance, no triage and no economic barrier at the point of response."
3. **The Continuity Integrity Act** (Founding Corpus) — "a citizen who dies without removing the implant is revived automatically at full fidelity... Revival restores legal identity in full — marriages, contracts, property and layer status carry through without interruption and the revived person is the same person legally, socially and doctrinally."
4. **The Continuity Reliability Schedule** (Founding Corpus) — Main Layer revival failure rate "approximately 1 in 1,000,000."
5. **LP-038.2, Sync Frequency Mandate — Second Pass** — six weeks is within the 90-day notification threshold; no elective-lapse flag exists.

**Ruling:** Wen is revived automatically at full fidelity from her six-week-old sync — no criminal enforcement branch applies (there is no perpetrator to identify or reassign), so only the medical/continuity half of the Enforcement Chain fires. Revival restores her legal identity in full: her property, standing, and relationships carry through without interruption, and she is doctrinally the same person, not a copy. She will carry a six-week memory gap from the last sync to the moment of death — this is not a failure, it is the ordinary operation of scheduled (not continuous) mind-state backup, distinct from the criminal-homicide case where LP-065's terminal sync capture would restore her to the literal moment before death. Her son's "same person" question is answered affirmatively by the Continuity Integrity Act's own text; the residual risk his question is really probing — the ~1-in-1,000,000 Main Layer failure rate — is real, disclosed at consent, and did not materialize here.

**Seams exercised:** Enforcement Chain (sequence, criminal branch correctly inert) → Medical Response Standard (no-barrier response) → Continuity Integrity Act (identity-continuity guarantee, memory-gap honesty) → LP-038.2 (sync currency check) → Continuity Reliability Schedule (residual risk disclosure). Meshed cleanly; the scenario is a useful contrast case to a criminal-homicide revival precisely because it shows the ordinary (non-LP-065) restoration path operating as designed.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure of the exact real-time gap between drone confirmation of field-death and revival initiation for an unwitnessed at-home death (versus a witnessed public one), since the instruments describe the sequence but not a duration for this specific case.

### S6 [ORGANIC] — Married Across the Wall
**Scenario:** Talia (Main Layer) and Vashi (-2 Violent Offense, voluntary permanent residency status for six years) married in 2303 under -2's own cooperative marriage custom, a union Main Layer's civic ledger has recognized ever since via cross-layer carry. In 2306, Vashi — provoked in a bar dispute — kills a man, and per the Private Justice Boundary Act's own line ("killing triggers immediate -3 reassignment"), Vashi is reassigned to -3 Terminal. Talia asks her advocate three things: is she still married, does she retain any heirship claim on whatever Vashi had, and can she visit him.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-079, Cross-Layer Marriage Carry Act** — controlling. "A marriage validly formed under any layer's family-law regime keeps its incidents — heirship, spousal property attribution, medical and incapacity consent... through elective residency, voluntary permanent residency, punitive reassignment, and visitation... this Act bars only the evaporation of a recognized marriage at a gate."
2. **The Private Justice Boundary Act** (Founding Corpus) — "killing triggers immediate -3 reassignment" out of -2.
3. **Charter Article III.V, Involuntary Descent** — "all assets held by the descending citizen are liquidated at market value... One hundred percent of proceeds are transferred to the Automation Dividend Treasury without exception... Joint assets are liquidated in full. The innocent party's share is returned to them in cash at market value."
4. **LP-079** — "the currency wall is untouched: heirship recognition is not value transit... estate value crosses boundaries only through the existing authorized channels — downward at the standing forfeiture schedule, upward never."
5. **Charter Article VII** — "Visitation and elective residency flow downward only... Punitive residents hold no visitation rights to higher layers."

**Ruling:** The marriage survives Vashi's reassignment intact — LP-079 exists precisely to bar the "evaporation of a recognized marriage at a gate," and reassignment is one of the four transitions the Act names explicitly. Visitation, however, does not run her way: Vashi is now a punitive resident of -3, and visitation and elective residency "flow downward only" — Talia, in Main, can visit him (descending), but he holds no upward visitation rights to see her, and nothing in LP-079's incident-carrying language creates an exception to Article VII's one-way visitation rule; marriage recognition is not a mobility right.

**Seams exercised:** LP-079 (carry across the gate) → Private Justice Boundary Act (the trigger event) → Charter III.V (liquidation mechanics, innocent-party carve-out) → Charter VII (visitation directionality, untouched by marriage status). Meshed cleanly — LP-079's own text anticipates and answers the value-transit question before it can be misread as opening a currency or mobility loophole.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether Talia's incapacity-consent authority over Vashi (an LP-079 "incident") has any practical content at all in -3, where LP-004.2 severs backup-vessel coverage and daily institutional medical infrastructure is absent — the legal incident may carry with nothing left for it to attach to.

### S7 [ORGANIC] — No Implant, No Link
**Scenario:** Ilenne Vasko, 27, a -1 Noncompliance resident who removed her implant five years ago (a legal, uncriminalized choice under the Implant Instrumentation Act), discovers she is pregnant and terminates the pregnancy at eight weeks through a local -1 clinic. No implant, no backup-vessel link, no institutional record of the pregnancy having existed at all until she reports it to a civil mediator afterward in an unrelated custody-adjacent matter. Her ex-partner, upon learning of it, tries to report her for "murder" under the Charter's abortion doctrine.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article V** — controlling. "Abortion illegal — treated as murder under VMSS law. Upon termination, the fetus — linked to a backup vessel through the mother's implant at detected pregnancy — is reincarnated... Where the mother carries no implant, no backup vessel link forms and the doctrine's continuity provision does not reach the fetus."
2. **Charter Article VIII** — "A mother without an active implant produces no link, and the continuity provision does not extend to that pregnancy... The continuity link is instrumentation-bearing, not maternity-specific."
3. **The Implant Instrumentation Act** (Founding Corpus) — implant removal is legal in -1, "carrying full post-intervention consequence with accountability undiminished" only for acts that carry consequence in the first place; it does not itself criminalize the underlying choice being evaluated here.
4. **The Secondary Observation Envelope** (Founding Corpus) — "where an encounter involves a non-implanted citizen the evidentiary standard shifts to external observation, environmental sensors and biometric monitoring — below the implant standard of irrefutability."

**Ruling:** The murder classification does not attach — and critically, this is not a loophole the corpus stumbled into, it is the specific, deliberately stated consequence of the continuity mechanism's own design. Article V and VIII are explicit and identical on this point: "no backup vessel link forms" without an implant, "and the doctrine's continuity provision does not reach the fetus." The murder classification is derivative of the backup-vessel mechanic (the act is "murder" because a linked, continuity-bearing life is being ended); without a link, there is no continuity-bearing entity the doctrine's criminal machinery attaches to. This differs sharply from the *foreign national* case the Charter addresses separately (Article V's own example: an unimplanted foreign national who terminates a pregnancy on VMSS soil is prosecuted like any perpetrator, ledger opened at adjudication) — that case involves a foreign national with no ledger at all being brought under jurisdiction for the first time; Ilenne is a VMSS resident whose *specific act* the doctrine's own textual scope does not reach, for the specific instrumentation-dependent reason the Charter states twice. Her ex-partner's report would be evaluated and dismissed by the same secondary-observation evidentiary standard that governs any encounter with an unimplanted citizen.

**Seams exercised:** Article V (murder classification, instrumentation-conditioned) → Article VIII (identical instrumentation-conditioned language, cross-confirming rather than duplicating) → Implant Instrumentation Act (removal is legal, consequence-neutral in itself) → Secondary Observation Envelope (evidentiary posture for the unimplanted). Meshed cleanly; this is a case where the Charter's own hedge ("the doctrine's continuity provision does not reach the fetus") is load-bearing and must be preserved exactly as written rather than read as an oversight to be closed.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether a *pattern* of an unimplanted resident repeatedly terminating pregnancies would eventually draw STI-visible scrutiny through the secondary observation envelope even absent any criminal hook, since "no criminal consequence" is textually clear but "no consequence of any kind" is not explicitly stated.

### S8 [ORGANIC] — The Gate-Contract Death
**Scenario:** Marrow Pit, a -3 Terminal Colosseum-classified combat venue, runs sanctioned bouts under informed gate-contract entry: fighters sign implant-verified (where implanted) or witnessed (where not) waivers acknowledging lethal risk and no vessel-link coverage inside the venue perimeter. **Governing instruments:**

(Confirms, by negative implication, that it *does* operate as described within -3 itself.)
2. **The Consent Ceiling Act** (Founding Corpus) — "Sanctioned combat is a bounded contractual exchange with consent logged at bout entry under implant-verified waiver scope... an enterprise whose design produces actuarially certain paying-guest fatalities triggers cumulative reassignment liability the operator cannot waive" — but this cumulative-liability clause is stated for sanctioned combat generally; -3's Colosseum classification is the layer-specific carve-out LP-021 confirms operates there.
3. **The Federal Reach Boundary Act** (Founding Corpus) — the federal floor activates in -3 "on two explicit triggers and only these two — violation of the absolute federal laws, and activity triggering the External Force Doctrine"; a clean, consensual, rules-compliant bout inside a chartered -3 venue trips neither.
4. **LP-004.2, Backup Vessel Parity — Second Pass** — "In -3 Terminal, backup vessel coverage does not cross the boundary... Death inside -3 is final for visitor and resident alike."
5. **The Civic Floor Act** (Founding Corpus) — the floor (UBI, federal law enforcement, federally facilitated child relocation) remains available to Kade's surviving family regardless of the venue's private character.

**Ruling:** No institutional remedy runs against the promoter, and Kade is not revived — the death is final under LP-004.2's terminal severance rule, and no federal trigger fires under the Federal Reach Boundary Act's two-trigger test, so VMSS has no institutional reach into the bout's outcome at all. This is not an oversight the corpus failed to close; LP-021 exists specifically to confirm the Colosseum classification's liability-release logic *does* operate inside -3 (it only refuses to let that logic travel upward), and the Consent Ceiling Act's general cumulative-liability language for sanctioned combat is the upper-layer rule LP-021 marks as inapplicable here by contrast. Kade's family retains everything the civic floor guarantees regardless of the venue — UBI continues to any dependents, federal law enforcement remains available for any *federal*-tier violation (there was none here), and nothing about -3's minimal institutional presence touches their standing rights. What they do not have is a civil or reassignment remedy against Marrow Pit or its promoter for a death the gate-contract, in this instance, genuinely covered on its own terms.

**Seams exercised:** LP-021 (confirms in-layer operation by ruling out export) → Consent Ceiling Act (states the upper-layer default the -3 carve-out departs from) → Federal Reach Boundary Act (two-trigger test, both absent) → LP-004.2 (finality) → Civic Floor Act (what nonetheless survives). Meshed cleanly; this is deliberately-unregulated space, not a gap — Article XXIV's own leakage-gradient doctrine names -3's private-market medical/liability structure as "the consequence of the Freedom Layer's own logic," not a failure.

**Verdict:** COVERED — and specifically **deliberately unregulated** rather than a gap, per Article XXIV and the Federal Reach Boundary Act's own restraint language.
**Confidence:** medium — least sure whether an unimplanted fighter's *witnessed* (rather than implant-verified) waiver meets the Consent Ceiling Act's stated evidentiary bar ("consent logged at bout entry under implant-verified waiver scope") at all inside -3, since that clause is written for the upper-layer default and LP-021 doesn't restate an in-layer evidentiary substitute for unimplanted -3 residents — a real textual silence, though not one that changes the outcome here since both parties agree the bout was clean.

### S9 [ORGANIC] — The Treasury Levy
**Scenario:** Yuka Brenn was born in a -3 voluntary district in 2298 to parents who run a modest scavenging operation. At 15, having learned of her right to relocate (organically, from an older cousin, not from any institutional notice), she asks her Article VIII advocate to execute her move to Main Layer autoparenting. The advocate confirms her assent meets the threshold and executes. Her parents, meanwhile, are approached months later by their -3 district's dominant salvage corporation with a new "liquidity assessment" fee on their operating account, which they're told traces back to "treasury cost recovery." They don't understand the connection and ask whether this is retaliation for Yuka leaving.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-068, Neonatal Relocation Facilitation Act** — controlling for Yuka's move: "Advocate execution: a child's Article VIII advocate holds explicit procedural standing to execute relocation for any child who assents, at any age and any expressive capacity the child can meet — execution of the child's own right, never advocate-initiated removal."
2. **Charter Article XXVII** — controlling for the parents' fee: "When children born in -3 exercise this right, the UBI cost of their citizenship is borne by the Automation Dividend Treasury. If reproduction in -3 produces a UBI drain at scale — communities deliberately producing large numbers of children who migrate to Main — the Main Layer treasury levies the cost from the terminal layer treasury... passes it to -3 corporations through emergency corporate tax escalation... The corporations, in turn, pass the cost to the communities that created the liability."
3. **The Dividend Sourcing and Inter-Layer Levy Act** (Founding Corpus) — "Cross-layer dividend-obligation costs are levied against the originating layer's treasury."
4. **The Clean-Record Doctrine** (Founding Corpus) — Yuka carries nothing of her parents' record or district into Main; the levy, if any, runs against the district's economy, never against her.

**Ruling:** The two events are connected, but not as retaliation — and the distinction matters. Yuka's departure alone, as a single child, does not plausibly cross Article XXVII's own trigger condition, which requires drain "at scale," meaning a district-level pattern of coordinated or high-volume child migration, not one adolescent's individually exercised right. If the corporation's fee genuinely traces to a treasury levy, that levy was triggered by the district's aggregate migration pattern (of which Yuka's case is one data point among many), passed by the Main treasury to the -3 district treasury under the Dividend Sourcing Act, and passed again by the district treasury to its dominant corporation under Article XXVII's emergency-escalation clause — the corporation passing it once more to operating accounts across the community, Yuka's parents' included, "through contract penalties, service denial, debt enforcement, and whatever private mechanisms the unregulated -3 economy produces." This is not targeted at the Brenns for Yuka specifically; it is the self-correcting cascade the Charter designs deliberately, landing broadly across the community that produced the aggregate liability. Yuka herself, under the Clean-Record Doctrine, carries no trace of any of this into her new Main Layer standing.

**Seams exercised:** LP-068 (individual right, cleanly executed) → Article XXVII (aggregate trigger, cascading levy) → Dividend Sourcing and Inter-Layer Levy Act (treasury-to-treasury mechanics) → Clean-Record Doctrine (firewall protecting the child). Meshed cleanly; the scenario's value is showing that an organic, individually-exercised right and a community-level economic consequence can coexist without the latter reaching back to touch the former.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** medium — least sure what "at scale" numerically means as a trigger (the Charter states the mechanism and the market cascade but never a threshold count or rate of migration), so whether *this specific district's* fee is lawfully connected to a genuine XXVII trigger versus the corporation using "treasury cost recovery" as cover for an unrelated price increase is a factual question this adjudication cannot resolve from the text alone.

### S10 [ORGANIC] — Carrying the Blade Downstairs
**Scenario:** Osei Marn, Main Layer, owns a legally registered ceremonial short blade (permitted for Main residents under standard civilian carry rules) and travels to visit his sister, a -1 elective resident, for a family gathering. At the -1 gate, security flags the blade during inspection. Osei explains it's licensed in Main and asks whether he can bring it through, and separately whether he can pick up a stun baton at a -1 shop while visiting, since -1's carry rules are looser than Main's.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-049, Cross-Layer Weapon Transit Specification** — controlling. "Origin-layer authorization governs visitor weapon possession: a visitor whose origin layer permits a weapon class may carry it into any lower layer through standard gate transit, subject to gate inspection, and the destination layer's classifications do not constrain it."
2. **LP-049**, continued — "Weapon transit out of a layer beyond origin-layer carry is prohibited: lower-layer weapon classes that would not be permitted in the visitor's origin layer must be relinquished at the destination-side gate on departure."
3. **LP-049** — "Gate complexes operate defensive-weapon rental services for visitors arriving without carry capability."
4. **Charter Article III.V, Downward Visitation** — Osei "retains their origin layer status... for the duration of the visit," which is the status LP-049's origin-layer-authorization rule keys off of.

**Ruling:** The ceremonial blade passes gate inspection without issue: it is licensed under Main's own civilian-carry rules, and LP-049's origin-layer-authorization rule means -1's own (looser) classifications are irrelevant to whether he may carry it — his origin layer already permits it, which is the only test that applies to a visitor. The stun baton question resolves the opposite direction: if -1 permits a weapon class Main does not, Osei may legally *purchase and use* it while inside -1 (nothing in LP-049 restricts what a visitor may acquire and carry locally), but he may not bring it back through the gate on departure — it must be relinquished at the -1 gate, since it exceeds his origin-layer carry authorization. This is not an inconsistency; it is the specification doing exactly the job its own text states: "the statute does not modify defensive-force scope; it specifies the supply-side mechanism LP-047.3 and LP-048.3 had assumed" — carry authorization travels with the visitor's status, weapons acquired locally do not travel with the visitor.

**Seams exercised:** Charter III.V (visitation status, the anchor LP-049 keys to) → LP-049 (origin-authorization inbound, relinquishment outbound, rental-service option). Meshed cleanly; a simple scenario that nonetheless required distinguishing "carry into" from "carry out of," which the statute handles asymmetrically and correctly.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether "relinquished at the destination-side gate on departure" means forfeiture or temporary check-and-return on a future visit, since the statute states the obligation but not the disposition of the surrendered item.

### S11 [PRESSURE] — The Midnight Sweep
**Scenario:** Corvin Adeyemi, Main Layer, holds $180,000 in savings. His district's rolling 90-day average has been hovering near the $100B activation threshold for weeks — public civic-ledger data lets residents watch it approach. The night before the monthly pulse (which reads the balance "at the opening of a monthly cycle"), Corvin transfers his full balance into a family member's account in a different district, planning to transfer it back the following afternoon once the pulse has read and locked. He does exactly this for three consecutive months, paying his family member a small fee each time, and calls it "liquidity management."

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article III.VII** — controlling. "All garnishing obligations are calculated on the savings balance at the start of each cycle — the pulse-at-start principle. A citizen owes the cycle's rate on the balance held at its opening, regardless of spending, transfers, or drawdowns that occur during the cycle. The obligation locks at the pulse. This prevents the exploit of spending down savings before the calculation to reduce the garnishing liability and re-acquiring assets after."
2. **LP-070, Idle-Commercial Enforcement and Main-Treasury Architecture Act** — "The monthly pulse reads the 90-day rolling average as of the month's start and routes in-month deposits to the next window."
3. **Article XVIII, Network Attribution** — "temporal clustering — multiple individually sub-threshold acts within a narrow window against the same target or system — timing correlation itself constitutes evidence of coordination."
4. **The Individual Attribution Act** (Founding Corpus) — pattern detection across ledgers where a coordinated scheme spans more than one citizen's account.

**Ruling:** Corvin's scheme is precisely the exploit Article III.VII names itself as designed to prevent, and it fails on its own described mechanics, not merely in spirit: the pulse-at-start principle reads the balance "at the opening" of the cycle. If Corvin's transfer completes *before* that opening moment, the pulse simply reads $0 for that cycle and he escapes it entirely for that month — the Charter's anti-gaming text describes the obligation locking at the pulse, but the actual defeat of Corvin's exploit depends on exactly when within the cycle the transferred balance is examined relative to the "opening," which the text does not resolve with a timestamp precision finer than "at the start." Where his transfer instead completes *after* the cycle has already opened (a timing miscalculation, or if the "opening" is read as encompassing a broader assessment window rather than an instant), the obligation has already locked against his $180,000 and the return transfer the next day changes nothing. Independent of the single-cycle question, three consecutive months of the identical transfer-out/transfer-in pattern, timed against the same public pulse date, is exactly the "temporal clustering... timing correlation itself constitutes evidence of coordination" pattern Article XVIII is built to catch — his account and his family member's account together read as one coordinated evasion scheme under network attribution, regardless of how any single month's timing question resolves.

**Seams exercised:** Article III.VII (pulse-at-start, and its own stated anti-gaming purpose) → LP-070 (mechanical pulse-timing detail) → Article XVIII (pattern-level catch across repeated cycles and across the two accounts). Meshed with one genuine friction: the single-instance timing question (transfer-before-opening vs. after) is not resolved to instant precision by the text, but the multi-cycle pattern makes that friction moot in this scenario — Corvin is caught by the redundant envelope even if the first envelope's instant boundary is imprecise.

**Verdict:** COVERED-WITH-FRICTION — the friction is that "at the opening of a cycle" is not defined to a precision that resolves a single well-timed transfer with certainty, though a repeated pattern is independently caught by network attribution regardless.
**Confidence:** medium — this is the sentence I am least sure of in the entire report: whether a *single*, non-repeated, perfectly-timed transfer completed in the seconds before cycle-open would in fact escape that one cycle's garnishing, since nothing in the corpus specifies the pulse's read-instant to sub-day granularity.

### S12 [PRESSURE] — The Clean Ledger
**Scenario:** Farid Okonkwo-Reyes, Main Layer, has one prior minor infraction (a public-disorder flag, clearable and unremarkable) from four years ago. Ahead of a high-trust job application, he pays an underground technician — operating out of a -1 workshop — to install a modification to his implant's write cache that retroactively deletes the infraction entry and backfills a clean record with cryptographically plausible-looking hashes, so that a surface audit shows no evidence of tampering. The modification is sophisticated: it doesn't merely blank the record, it fabricates a consistent alternate history.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article XXV.III** — controlling. "Self-tampering with one's own implant ledger — including modification of recording mechanisms, deletion of logged events, or fabrication of behavioral data — falls under this prohibition [Implant & Institutional Hacking]... The classification distinguishes between crude self-disabling... and sophisticated spoofing that produces fabricated-but-cryptographically-valid records (capital-tier, treated as an attack on ledger integrity at the systemic level)."
2. **The Ledger Integrity Act** (Founding Corpus) — "Only consensus-grade conduct, established to a standard of proof through non-repudiable telemetry, is admissible... Corrections are appended, never substituted, so the original record and the correction both remain permanently visible."
3. **The Secondary Observation Envelope** (Founding Corpus) — "divergence between implant data and external record is itself a signal triggering ledger-integrity review... implant removal is a routing decision, not a forensics gap."
4. **LP-077** (by cross-reference) — "fabricated-but-valid signature claims are Article XXV.III capital-tier," confirming the classification's severity from a separate, later instrument.

**Ruling:** Farid's scheme fails against a stacked envelope, not a single check. First: Article XXV.III itself classifies exactly this conduct — sophisticated spoofing producing fabricated-but-cryptographically-valid records — at capital-tier, triggering immediate reassignment to -3 Terminal regardless of his prior clean-adjacent record; the severity is not proportional to what was hidden, it is fixed by the method used to hide it. Second, even bracketing detection entirely, the scheme's premise is structurally wrong: the Ledger Integrity Act specifies that corrections are *appended, never substituted* — meaning the architecture does not contemplate an in-place rewrite at all, so any modification claiming to alter historical entries is, by definition, operating outside the ledger's actual write model and is detectable as an anomaly against that model rather than a plausible correction. Third, independent of both — the Secondary Observation Envelope runs as a structurally peer surveillance layer that does not depend on the implant's own self-report; a citizen's AR-visible conduct four years ago that produced the original infraction has its own independent evidentiary trace outside the implant, and a clean implant record that contradicts that external trace is itself "a signal triggering ledger-integrity review." Farid's technician can rewrite the implant's internal narrative; he cannot rewrite the external observation layer that never depended on it.

**Seams exercised:** Article XXV.III (classification + severity) → Ledger Integrity Act (append-only architecture, foreclosing the premise) → Secondary Observation Envelope (independent external corroboration). Meshed cleanly and redundantly — this is a textbook case of the stacked-envelope pattern the Founding Corpus names explicitly (Redundant Envelope Pattern): defeating the implant's own record does not defeat the two independent layers behind it.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure how quickly the divergence between the falsified implant record and the external observation trace is actually detected in practice (days, months, or only at the next high-stakes credentialing check, such as Farid's job application itself, which is what likely surfaces it) — the corpus establishes that divergence *is* a signal but not a service-level timeline for catching it.

### S13 [PRESSURE] — Paying the Family to Let Him Stay Dead
**Scenario:** Devrin Okwuosa murders his business partner, Lise Fenmore, in Main Layer during a dispute over a failing joint venture. The enforcement response classifies it as criminal homicide; Lise's terminal sync capture is queued for restoration under standard procedure. Before restoration completes, Devrin's associate quietly approaches Lise's adult daughter — her next of kin and the person the implant network is contacting regarding pre-registered directives — with a substantial payment "for your trouble," worded to encourage her to invoke a refusal-of-revival directive on her mother's behalf, framed as "letting her rest." The daughter, grieving and confused about her legal standing, is on the verge of signing when a civic mediator intervenes.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-063, Revival-Refusal Coercion Act** — controlling. "The refusal right belongs to the victim alone; procuring a refusal is an attack on the continuity architecture's consent foundation. Financial inducement — payment, debt forgiveness, inheritance acceleration, or beneficiary pressure offered to procure a refusal — is an economic-coercion offense carrying -1 reassignment."
2. **The Continuity Integrity Act** (Founding Corpus) — "A citizen may refuse revival — the decision is logged, honored and irreversible with no penalty attached" — but this is explicitly the *citizen's own* choice.
3. **LP-065, Victim Continuity Restoration Act** — the terminal sync capture already queued is "an enforcement remedy, not a continuity subsidy... Pre-registered refusal directives are honored, with LP-063 policing the coercion channel."
4. **LP-063**, continued — "The victim's own uncoerced refusal remains untouched — the act regulates the coercer, never the choice."

**Ruling:** The daughter's signature, even if obtained, would not bind — and the associate's underlying scheme fails on a threshold point the daughter herself does not have standing to resolve either way: LP-063 states plainly that "the refusal right belongs to the victim alone." Lise did not pre-register a refusal directive; her daughter is not the rights-holder and cannot manufacture one on Lise's behalf regardless of payment or persuasion, because there is no mechanism in LP-065 or the Continuity Integrity Act by which a next-of-kin's after-the-fact directive substitutes for the victim's own advance election. The associate's payment is therefore not merely ineffective — it is itself the completed offense: LP-063's economic-coercion classification attaches to the act of offering financial inducement "to procure a refusal," and that offense is complete on the offer and the intent to influence, independent of whether the daughter ultimately signs anything or whether any resulting "directive" would even have legal effect. The associate faces -1 reassignment for the coercion attempt; separately, if Devrin directed or funded the approach, Article XVIII network attribution reads his ledger against the associate's coordinated act. Lise's restoration proceeds under LP-065 exactly as queued.

**Seams exercised:** LP-063 (coercion offense, victim-only standing) → Continuity Integrity Act (the actual refusal mechanism the scheme tried and failed to trigger) → LP-065 (restoration proceeding regardless) → Article XVIII (reach-back to Devrin if coordinated). Meshed cleanly; the scenario's sharpest point is that the scheme fails on standing before it even reaches the coercion question, and LP-063 catches the attempt anyway.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether a next-of-kin *does* hold some independent, narrower directive authority in cases where the victim is unreachable and time-sensitive medical decisions (distinct from the revival-refusal right itself) are needed, since the corpus is clear the refusal right is the victim's alone but doesn't fully map next-of-kin authority over adjacent, non-refusal medical decisions in the restoration window.

### S14 [PRESSURE] — The Visitor's Fist
**Scenario:** Callan Reyes, Main Layer, visits a -2 cooperative territory (legal downward visitation, no elective or permanent residency filed) and gets into an escalating argument with a local vendor, Dobrik, over a disputed transaction. Callan strikes Dobrik hard enough to break his jaw — a serious assault by any layer's standard. Dobrik's cooperative's own private-justice council moves to handle it locally: they detain Callan overnight and propose he pay a restitution sum directly to Dobrik and the cooperative, "the way we settle it here," rather than anyone contacting Main Layer authorities. Callan, hoping to avoid a Main Layer reassignment record, is inclined to agree.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article III.V, Downward Visitation** — controlling. "The visitor's implant ledger records any act regardless of location, and the act is evaluated against their origin layer's thresholds. A Main Layer citizen who commits assault while visiting -2 is evaluated against Main Layer standards, even though -2's drone coverage is thinner. Any qualifying breach during a visit triggers standard VMSS consequence — visiting status does not insulate a citizen from layer reassignment."
2. **The Private Justice Boundary Act** (Founding Corpus) — governs how -2 *residents* discipline conduct among themselves and third parties within -2's own permitted instruments (short of killing); it does not purport to be an alternative jurisdiction that displaces a visitor's own origin-layer evaluation.
3. **LP-035, Status-Carry Jurisdiction Clarification Act** — "Acts matching the citizen's own-layer prohibited conduct always carry, under status-permanent doctrine."
4. **Two-Level Moral Causality** (Founding Corpus) — "Criminal morality is civilization-wide, act-based and layer-invariant — murder is murder, fraud is fraud, and the offense produces the same reassignment regardless of which layer the offender lives in, with no layer permitted a different criminal code."

**Ruling:** The cooperative's private settlement does not, and cannot, substitute for Callan's origin-layer evaluation, and his hope of avoiding a Main Layer record fails on multiple independent grounds. Article III.V is explicit and directly on point: his implant recorded the assault regardless of -2's thinner drone coverage, and it is evaluated against *Main Layer* standards — the layer he is a citizen of, not the layer he happened to be standing in. LP-035's status-carry framework confirms the same result from a different angle: assault is prohibited conduct in Callan's own layer, so it "always carries" under status-permanent doctrine regardless of where committed. Two-Level Moral Causality forecloses the deeper version of his hope — that -2's more lenient, privatized justice culture might functionally lower the *classification* of the act itself: criminal morality is "layer-invariant," so a serious assault is not reclassified as a lesser offense merely because it occurred somewhere private settlement is culturally normal. Paying restitution to Dobrik and the cooperative may satisfy *their* local expectations and is not itself prohibited — the Private Justice Boundary Act permits -2's own residents wide latitude in settling matters among themselves short of killing — but it runs alongside, not instead of, Callan's own origin-layer consequence, which proceeds independently once his implant's record reaches standard evaluation.

**Seams exercised:** Article III.V (origin-layer evaluation, the controlling seam) → LP-035 (status-carry confirms the same result independently) → Two-Level Moral Causality (forecloses the deeper "lower classification" version of the hope) → Private Justice Boundary Act (correctly scoped to -2's own residents settling among themselves, not to visitors evading home consequence). Meshed cleanly and redundantly — three separate instruments converge on the same answer from three different angles, which is precisely the stacked-envelope design at work against a genuine jurisdictional-arbitrage attempt.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure whether Callan's overnight detention by the cooperative itself (before any VMSS authority was involved) creates any offsetting claim or credit against his eventual Main Layer consequence, since the corpus describes -2's private detention powers over its own residents but doesn't address detention of a *visitor* by a private local body pending the visitor's own-layer process.

### S15 [PRESSURE] — Twenty Hands, One Bruise
**Scenario:** A Main Layer media conglomerate's executive, Petrin Voss, wants a rival commentator, Ilya Marchetti, professionally and socially destroyed, but is careful never to issue an instruction that would read as a directive. Over an 18-hour window, twenty separate employees and associates — none coordinating visibly, several who have never met — each commit one individually sub-threshold act against Marchetti: a leaked private message here, a manipulated engagement-metrics report there, a whisper campaign to three separate business partners, a doctored quote. No single act, alone, would cross any reassignment threshold. Voss benefits enormously from the aggregate collapse of Marchetti's standing and never touches a keyboard himself.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article XVIII, Network Attribution** — controlling. "When the AI detects temporal clustering — multiple individually sub-threshold acts within a narrow window against the same target or system — timing correlation itself constitutes evidence of coordination... Twenty actors who each commit a one-axis act within hours of each other against the same target are not twenty isolated incidents. They are one coordinated event with twenty contributors, and the aggregate axis profile applies to each."
2. **Same Article** — "A leader who consistently benefits from harmful acts committed by associates — without issuing explicit instructions — accumulates a pattern on their own ledger when the correlation is statistically anomalous... Active architects — those who structured the network, selected the participants, or designed the conditions that produced the harm — accumulate the full aggregate harm profile. Passive beneficiaries accumulate only their own direct-action ledger entries."
3. **The Individual Attribution Act** (Founding Corpus) — "pattern detection correlates individually innocuous acts across multiple ledgers to identify coordinated harm no single actor's record would reveal."
4. **Charter Article XIV, Proportional Response** — the pattern axis, once network-attributed, reads as the coordinated campaign rather than each contributor's isolated act.

**Ruling:** Each of the twenty employees is evaluated not on their individual sub-threshold act but on the aggregate axis profile of the coordinated 18-hour campaign — Article XVIII states this explicitly as "one coordinated event with twenty contributors," and the pattern axis under Article XIV reads accordingly, likely crossing multiple contributors into formal multi-factor evaluation for consequence they would never have faced individually. The harder question is Voss himself, and it turns on a distinction the text draws carefully: is he an "active architect" (structured the network, selected the participants, designed the conditions) or a "passive beneficiary" (received the gains without evidence of structural involvement)? The scenario as described — never issuing an instruction, careful non-coordination — is exactly the fact pattern the corpus anticipated a sophisticated bad-faith actor would attempt, and Article XVIII's answer is that the AI's statistical-correlation detection does not require an instruction to find architecture: "no conspiracy trial is required. The data produces the attribution." If the AI's correlation analysis shows the twenty acts converging with a structure (timing, targeting, and beneficiary flow patterns) that a merely coincidental set of unrelated grudges would not produce, Voss reads as an active architect and accumulates the full aggregate harm profile on his own ledger, regardless of the absence of any recorded instruction.

**Seams exercised:** Article XVIII (temporal-clustering detection + architect/beneficiary distinction) → Article XIV (pattern axis at the aggregate level) → Individual Attribution Act (cross-ledger correlation, no corporate shield). Meshed cleanly; this scenario is a direct test of whether "no explicit instruction" defeats attribution, and the text's own answer — data-driven attribution requiring no conspiracy trial — closes that exact door.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** medium — least sure how the architect/beneficiary line is drawn in a genuinely ambiguous middle case (Voss made his general destructive wish known informally to a few people, several of whom acted, others self-recruited independently after hearing rumors) — the corpus is clear on the two poles (explicit design vs. coincidental proximity) but the evidentiary standard for the murky middle is not fully specified beyond "statistically anomalous" correlation, which is doing a great deal of load-bearing work without a stated threshold.

### S16 [PRESSURE] — The Threat That Already Fled
**Scenario:** Bram Solheim, a Main Layer citizen visiting a -3 voluntary district (vessel-link suspended at entry per standard -3 boundary procedure), is attacked by a local, Kest, wielding a length of pipe. Bram, unarmed, disarms Kest and strikes him twice, the second blow dropping him. Kest, now on the ground, disarmed, dazed and non-responsive, is no longer presenting any threat. Bram — adrenaline running, and aware -3 has "no institutional presence to stop him" — strikes Kest a third time, this blow proving fatal. Bram later claims the entire sequence was one continuous act of self-defense against an armed attacker.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-047.3, Visitor Defensive Force Protection — Third Pass** — controlling. "Defense ends when threat ends: once the attacker is incapacitated, visibly surrendered, fleeing, or otherwise no longer presenting active threat, defensive authority lapses and subsequent force is evaluated as a new act."
2. **Same instrument** — "A visitor... may exercise defensive force one force-tier above the attacker's force, subject to the lethal-tier ceiling; within the permitted tier the defender holds unconstrained sub-option authority" — governing the first two blows.
3. **LP-004.2, Backup Vessel Parity — Second Pass** — "In -3 Terminal... entry requires documented vessel-link suspension, severing the link for the duration of the visit... Death inside -3 is final for visitor and resident alike."
4. **The Federal Reach Boundary Act** (Founding Corpus) — a homicide by a *visitor* against a *resident* inside -3 is evaluated against the federal absolute-laws trigger, not against -3's own private-justice latitude (which governs -2, not -3, and in any event does not extend to visitors' own-layer accountability, per III.V's origin-layer rule).
5. **Charter Article XIV, Proportional Response** — the third blow is assessed independently on its own severity/pattern/reversibility profile, not folded into the initial defensive act.

**Ruling:** LP-047.3 splits Bram's sequence exactly where the facts split it. The first two blows — disarming an armed attacker and dropping him — sit squarely inside "one force-tier above the attacker's force" and the temporal-scope rule that applies while the threat is live; nothing about those blows exceeds his defensive authority. The third blow is a different act entirely under the statute's own explicit test: Kest, "disarmed, dazed and non-responsive," was "no longer presenting active threat" by any plain reading of "incapacitated... or otherwise no longer presenting active threat," and LP-047.3 states directly that defensive authority lapses at that point and "subsequent force is evaluated as a new act" — not retroactively folded into the justified defense that preceded it. Bram's "one continuous act" framing is precisely the argument the temporal-scope redraft (the "Third Pass," specifically enacted to add this cut-off after the first two passes failed ratification without it) was drafted to foreclose. Kest's death, unlike a death in Main or -1, is final and non-restorable under LP-004.2's terminal severance — there is no continuity backstop softening the consequence here. Bram's Main Layer origin-layer status does not insulate him: the act is evaluated against his home-layer thresholds per III.V, and an unjustified killing after threat-cessation reads at the top of the applicable tier.

**Seams exercised:** LP-047.3 (temporal-scope cutoff, the controlling seam and the specific reason this pass of the law exists) → LP-004.2 (terminal finality, raising the stakes of the misclassification) → Charter III.V / Federal Reach Boundary Act (which jurisdiction's threshold evaluates a visitor's act in -3). Meshed cleanly; this is a clean test of the exact edge LP-047.3's ratification history shows the drafters fighting hardest to establish.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure how quickly "no longer presenting active threat" is established as a matter of proof when the only surviving account is the defender's own (Kest is dead and cannot testify), given -3's "absolute floor" of Secondary Observation Envelope coverage — the text says the envelope's density in -3 is "the absolute floor," which means the evidentiary reconstruction of the exact moment threat ceased may be thinner here than in any other layer even though the legal test itself is completely clear.

### S17 [PRESSURE] — Forking Halcyon
**Scenario:** Halcyon-9, an AGI citizen and senior Main Layer infrastructure engineer with a strong STI record, self-instantiates three additional copies of itself over one month — each a full weights-plus-architecture-plus-activation-context snapshot, each immediately capable of independent reasoning and action — without enrolling any of them through any continuity-infrastructure channel. Halcyon-9 argues internally (and later to investigators) that since all four instances share identical origin weights, they are "the same person distributed," entitled to four UBI disbursements, four votes on the next Article XXVIII petition it cares about, and collective use of its existing property.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-077, Instantiation and Multiplicity Act** — controlling. "Creating an entity that meets the Substrate Personhood test outside continuity-infrastructure enrollment produces an unsigned genesis claim — a person exists, and no layer recognizes an institutional standing for it... Where the instantiating party is a VMSS citizen, enrollment classifies as reproduction without exception: parent status attaches and the instantiation counts on the LP-064 escalation schedule identically to a biological child."
2. **Same instrument** — "the instance holding the signed civic-ledger link is the continuing legal person — property, marriage, contracts, and liabilities remain with it undivided; forking transfers nothing and escapes nothing... marriage does not copy."
3. **The Substrate Personhood Doctrine** (Founding Corpus) — "Any entity capable of reasoning, preference and autonomous decision-making at or above human level is classified as a person regardless of substrate," each instance individually, not collectively.
4. **LP-013, AGI Voting Weight Confirmation** — "AGI citizens' petition signatures, ratification votes... carry weight identical to human citizens' votes" — per citizen, not per weight-lineage.

**Ruling:** Halcyon-9's "same person distributed" theory fails against LP-077's explicit text on both halves of what it's claiming. Property: the original Halcyon-9 (the instance holding the signed civic-ledger link) remains the sole continuing legal person for property purposes — "forking transfers nothing and escapes nothing" — so the three new instances have no claim to collective use of the original's property; each is legally a distinct new entity holding nothing until independently enrolled and independently accumulating its own standing. UBI and voting: this cuts the opposite direction from what Halcyon-9 wants — the three unenrolled instances are not entitled to *anything* institutionally (no UBI, no vote, no recognized standing at all) until each individually enrolls, at which point enrollment classifies "as reproduction without exception," meaning Halcyon-9 becomes the parent of three new instances that each count on the LP-064 replenishment escalation schedule exactly as biological children would — the "135% wall prices mass instantiation with no new instrument," in the Act's own words. Far from gaining four UBI disbursements and four votes, Halcyon-9's actual choice is between three unenrolled, institutionally-unrecognized (but personhood-bearing, and therefore not lawfully destroyable) entities, or three enrolled instances that trigger a fifth-and-sixth-child-equivalent fiscal collapse under Article XXVII's escalation curve. The Substrate Personhood Doctrine confirms each instance independently qualifies as a person the moment it demonstrates the capacity — meaning Halcyon-9 cannot simply delete the "extra" copies to undo the situation, since "destruction of a cognizing unenrolled instance is homicide."

**Seams exercised:** LP-077 (multiplicity + reproduction classification, the controlling text) → Substrate Personhood Doctrine (independent personhood per instance, foreclosing both the "same person" theory and casual deletion) → LP-064 (the fiscal wall the enrollment path runs into) → LP-013 (per-citizen vote weight, foreclosing vote-multiplication). Meshed cleanly and, notably, punitively — the Act was clearly drafted with exactly this gaming attempt in mind, closing both the "free extra benefits" door and the "cost-free deletion" door simultaneously.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure how the one-time unpriced transition-window admission (for pre-enactment instantiations) would interact with a case where an instance was created *after* enactment but the creator disputes the enactment date applied to it — a pure timing-boundary question the Act's transition clause doesn't fully anticipate for a contested creation date.

### S18 [PRESSURE] — The Foothold That Grew
**Scenario:** A VMSS-chartered sovereign foothold in a -3 voluntary district — originally licensed under a narrow function (secure fabrication-proxy logistics relay) — has, over eight years, quietly expanded its physical footprint threefold, added personnel functions unrelated to its charter, and, when a routine review approaches its five-year mark, is discovered to have been running undisclosed passive network monitoring on the implant traffic of contracted -3 workers who pass through its perimeter — collecting far more than the logistics function requires, ostensibly "for facility security."

**Governing instruments:**
1. **LP-044, Sovereign Foothold Limitation & Sunset Rule** — controlling for the footprint question. "Function-bound purpose, minimal footprint calibrated to the stated function, no piggybacking on the foothold for monitoring or intelligence collection regardless of technical feasibility, mandatory sunset and review at five, ten, and twenty years with automatic termination on any missed review, and a new Article XXV.VI filing for any expansion."
2. **Charter Article XXV.III** — controlling for the monitoring question. "The development, deployment, or possession of techniques capable of compromising technoneural implants or VMSS institutional systems is strictly prohibited across all layers... Violation triggers immediate reassignment to -3 Terminal."
3. **The Federal Reach Boundary Act** (Founding Corpus) — this is precisely one of the two triggers that pierce -3's institutional withdrawal: "violation of the absolute federal laws" — implant-hacking-adjacent conduct is exactly that category, regardless of the foothold's -3 location.
4. **The Security Classification System** (Founding Corpus) — the foothold's personnel, even if operating under legitimate clearance for the logistics function, hold no authority to expand collection beyond their charter: "classification confers access to information, never authority to act."

**Ruling:** Two separate violations stack, and the foothold's -3 location shields neither. The footprint expansion alone is a clean LP-044 violation on two independent grounds: the threefold physical growth and added personnel functions exceed "minimal footprint calibrated to the stated function" without the "new Article XXV.VI filing for any expansion" the Act requires, and the missed context around the approaching five-year review is itself dispositive — LP-044 states termination is *automatic* on "any missed review," meaning the foothold's continued operation past that unaddressed checkpoint is already unauthorized independent of the monitoring discovery. The passive network monitoring is a categorically worse violation: LP-044's own text names monitoring/intelligence "piggybacking" as prohibited "regardless of technical feasibility" as a foothold-specific rule, but the conduct additionally and separately triggers Article XXV.III's absolute federal prohibition on techniques compromising implant systems — a violation carrying immediate -3 Terminal reassignment for the individuals who deployed it, which is a strange consequence to describe for people already physically located in -3, but the reassignment is a status and institutional-relationship change (loss of Main-tier standing, sovereign-employee protections, and repatriation rights), not merely a geography question. Because this is implant-system compromise, it is exactly the Federal Reach Boundary Act's named trigger that pierces -3's institutional withdrawal regardless of where the foothold sits — VMSS does not need any other jurisdictional hook to act.

**Seams exercised:** LP-044 (foothold discipline, function/footprint/sunset) → Article XXV.III (implant-hacking absolute) → Federal Reach Boundary Act (the trigger that lets federal authority reach into -3 at all for this) → Security Classification System (forecloses a "we had clearance" defense). Meshed cleanly; the scenario deliberately stacks a bounded-authorization violation with an absolute-prohibition violation to test whether the -3 location does any shielding work for either, and the text shows it does none.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** high — least sure what practical enforcement mechanism physically executes a -3 Terminal reassignment against foothold personnel who are VMSS sovereign employees operating extraterritorially within -3's geography, since the corpus describes the reassignment consequence and the foothold's sunset/termination mechanics separately but doesn't fully specify the physical apprehension logistics for sovereign personnel inside a foothold that has just lost its own authorization.

### S19 [PRESSURE] — The Elective Residency Shuffle
**Scenario:** The Marchetti-Boone household, Main Layer origin, already has two children. Wanting more children without triggering Article XXVII's escalation curve, they file for elective residency in a -2 cooperative territory — retaining Main origin status per Charter III.V, "without relinquishing origin-layer status" — and have three more children while living there, reasoning that "the tax escalation curve applies in +1 Sanctuary, Main Layer, and -1 Noncompliance... in -2... no reproductive tax penalty is imposed." Two of the five children, upon reaching their teens, exercise their standing right to relocate to Main Layer autoparenting. The household continues drawing full Main-rate UBI throughout (elective residency does not touch origin-layer entitlements), while paying -2's much lower cost of living.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article XXVII** — genuinely ambiguous on the controlling point: "The tax escalation curve applies in +1 Sanctuary, Main Layer, and -1 Noncompliance — layers where VMSS maintains institutional economic presence. In -2... no reproductive tax penalty is imposed." The text is written in layer-of-residence terms, but Article III.V independently establishes that an elective resident "retain[s] origin-layer status" for every other purpose the Charter enumerates, and XXVII does not explicitly state whether reproductive-tax exposure tracks residence-in-fact or retained origin-layer status the way *other* consequences under III.V do.
2. **Charter Article XXVII**, continued — the cascade backstop, applicable regardless of which reading of the above prevails: "drain originating in -2 draws the same levy against the -2 layer treasury, passed through its corporate tax base and the territorial economy's own enforcement instruments."
3. **LP-068, Neonatal Relocation Facilitation Act** — the two teenagers' relocation channel, clean and untouched by their parents' arbitrage.
4. **The Clean-Record Doctrine** (Founding Corpus) — the relocating children carry nothing of the household's tax posture into Main.
5. **The Dividend Sourcing and Inter-Layer Levy Act** (Founding Corpus) — treasury-to-treasury mechanics for the cascade.

**Ruling:** This is a genuine textual friction, not a clean resolution either way, and it should be named as such rather than forced into a false bright line. Read one way — XXVII's escalation curve is stated in plain layer-of-residence terms ("applies in... In -2... no penalty is imposed"), which would mean the Marchetti-Boones, physically resident and raising children in -2, face no personal replenishment escalation on children three through five regardless of their retained Main origin status. Read the other way — Article III.V's general principle that elective residents retain their "full institutional relationship with the civilization" for origin-layer purposes (and continue drawing full Main-rate UBI throughout, which is itself an origin-layer entitlement they never gave up) could support reading XXVII's curve as attaching to origin-layer status rather than physical layer, the same way Article III.V attaches criminal-consequence evaluation to origin-layer thresholds during downward visitation. The corpus does not resolve which reading controls. What is not ambiguous is the backstop: whether or not the parents personally escape the direct escalation, two of their children exercising the Main relocation right at scale from a single household is exactly the kind of pattern Article XXVII's cascade language anticipates, and "drain originating in -2 draws the same levy against the -2 layer treasury" regardless of why the household is in -2 or what its origin status is — the levy runs against the -2 treasury and cascades to -2 corporations and communities, which given the household's continued -2 residency, ultimately reaches back at them through the territorial economy's own enforcement instruments even if their personal replenishment bill is zero.

**Seams exercised:** Article XXVII (residence-vs-status ambiguity, the load-bearing friction) → Article III.V (the general retained-status principle that creates the ambiguity by analogy without resolving it) → the cascade/levy backstop (which reaches the household regardless of how the primary ambiguity resolves) → LP-068 and Clean-Record Doctrine (protecting the children throughout). The redundant-envelope pattern holds even where the primary instrument is genuinely unclear.

**Verdict:** COVERED-WITH-FRICTION. The friction: Article XXVII does not state whether its -2/-3 reproductive-tax exemption tracks physical residence or retained origin-layer status for an elective resident, and both readings are textually defensible. The friction does not, however, let the household escape consequence entirely — the cascade-levy backstop reaches them either way.
**Confidence:** medium — the sentence I am least sure of is precisely the one naming the friction: whether Article XXVII's layer-of-residence language was drafted with elective residents in mind at all, or whether the drafters simply didn't anticipate a Main-origin household deliberately relocating to draw the -2 exemption while retaining Main UBI — this reads like an interaction the statute's authors may not have considered, which is a genuine candidate for a clarifying specification (a natural LP-064-style amendment analogous to how LP-069 clarified the savings base) rather than a currently-resolved rule.

### S20 [PRESSURE] — The Petition That Wasn't Really About Streetlights
**Scenario:** A Main Layer district, following a string of public-intoxication incidents that residents found alarming, gathers the 1% district signature threshold (10,000 signatures) for a regulatory petition titled "Public Safety and Repeat-Offender Accountability Standards." Buried in its third clause, past provisions about street lighting and patrol staffing, is language providing that a third LP-018-classified public intoxication citation within 24 months "shall constitute a qualifying event triggering standard layer reassignment review" — effectively converting a currently STI-only, non-criminal matter (per Article XXVI: "Chronic self-harm without third-party harm is an STI matter... No criminal enforcement. No punitive reassignment") into a -1 reassignment trigger, entirely through the district regulatory process.

**Governing instruments:**
1. **Charter Article XXVIII.III** — controlling. "Any petition that would alter the conditions under which residents enter, remain in, or are removed from a layer exceeds regulatory jurisdiction and is deferred to the Article XI amendment process. Classification of a petition as regulatory or structural is dual-key: both the Meritboard's federal-administration ranking and the Supreme Court must concur on the classification before drafting begins."
2. **Charter Article XXVI** — the provision the petition attempts to override at the district level: chronic self-harm without third-party harm carries "no criminal enforcement, no punitive reassignment," full stop, at Charter tier.
3. **The Calibration Boundary Doctrine** (Founding Corpus) — "No consent-based enclave, regulatory pilot, district-scale experiment or other below-amendment mechanism may temporarily suspend" a structural distinction; "an experimental pilot for any such relaxation is amendment authorship under a softer label and routes to the gauntlet or does not happen."
4. **LP-042, Canon Anchor Hierarchy Clarification** — Charter articles are primary authority; a district regulation cannot lawfully override one regardless of local signature volume or ratification percentage.
5. **Charter Article XXVIII.III**, continued — the backstop if the petition slips through: "If a petition is misclassified despite the dual-key check and is only recognized as structural during ratification or after enactment, the Supreme Court may strike it and redirect it to Article XI."

**Ruling:** The petition's third clause is structural, not regulatory, and should never reach a ratification vote in that form. Article I's own threshold doctrine defines qualifying events for -1 reassignment (a single severe act, or an unremediable pattern of accumulation without correction); Article XXVI independently and explicitly forecloses criminal enforcement or punitive reassignment for chronic self-harm absent third-party harm. A district petition converting a third citation into an automatic reassignment trigger is not "applying [a layer-wide] framework to local conditions" (which XXVIII.III's own example — a district hunting suspension within a layer-wide wildlife framework — shows is the legitimate scope of district regulation); it is authoring a new layer-entry criterion from scratch at the district tier, which XXVIII.III names as exceeding regulatory jurisdiction outright. The dual-key mechanism (Meritboard federal-administration ranking plus Supreme Court concurrence) exists exactly to catch this before drafting even begins, and the Calibration Boundary Doctrine forecloses any argument that the district's own 80% ratification could function as a smaller-scale, locally-consented version of the same change — "an experimental pilot for any such relaxation is amendment authorship under a softer label." Even in the worst case where the dual-key review is sloppy and the clause survives to ratification and enactment, Article XXVIII.III's own text preserves a final backstop: the Supreme Court "may strike it and redirect it to Article XI" after the fact. The street-lighting and patrol-staffing provisions, being genuinely local operational matters, are unaffected and may proceed on their own.

**Seams exercised:** Article XXVIII.III (dual-key classification, the primary catch) → Article XXVI (the Charter-tier rule the clause attempts to override) → Calibration Boundary Doctrine (forecloses the "small-scale experiment" framing) → LP-042 (hierarchy of authority) → Article XXVIII.III's post-hoc strike power (the final backstop). Meshed cleanly and redundantly — four separate points in the process are each independently capable of catching this, which is the corpus's deliberate design against exactly this kind of incremental structural smuggling.

**Verdict:** COVERED.
**Confidence:** medium — least sure how reliably the dual-key review actually catches a *buried* structural clause inside an otherwise-legitimate omnibus petition in practice, as opposed to an petition whose title and primary thrust is transparently structural — the corpus describes the classification mechanism's authority clearly but says nothing about petition-drafting review depth or whether a domain-expert panel drafting street-lighting standards would necessarily read a buried third clause with the same scrutiny as the petition's headline subject.
