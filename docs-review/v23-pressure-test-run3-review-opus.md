## Adjudicated findings

### S19 (⚠ GAP — coerced revival-directive rescission): **NARROWED, approaching defeated**
The factual core verified: LP-063's offense schedule is textually one-directional (every enumerated tier is procurement-of-refusal, laws.html:1339), and a full-corpus sweep found no instrument tiering the mirror direction. But the steelman found the asymmetry **drafted, not accidental**: LP-063's violence-equivalent tier is expressly completion-conditioned ("where a procured refusal completes and the victim remains dead," laws.html:1340), and the mirror direction *cannot complete* — the honored refusal is irreversible (laws.html:1960). Whitepaper §17.1.6 (whitepaper.html:1068) shows canon treating LP-063 as refusal-scoped and closing the adjacent funding-lapse gap by *new statute* (LP-065) rather than broad reading. Importing LP-063's completed-homicide tier onto a structurally-impotent attempt would over-punish; generic Article XIV three-axis evaluation of the coercer's actual conduct (bribery, harassment, bad-faith filings) is the proportional envelope, backed by Article XVIII attribution ("the coercion is logged and survives the victim") and the Article XXI novelty filter. **The run's proposed bidirectional statute is not warranted.** Residual: one aggravation-calibration question (does targeting the continuity-consent foundation aggravate the generic evaluation toward LP-063's band?) — **folds into queue item 5**, which this finding independently rediscovered.

### S17 (friction — Sanctuary gladiator): **RESOLVED-BY-EXISTING-TEXT**
The run's two-track ruling (no reassignment; catastrophic rating consequence + phasing) is affirmatively compelled, not just implied. The clause the run under-weighted: Two-Level Moral Causality's "**no layer permitted a different criminal code**" (laws.html:1878) — reading III.V to let Sanctuary criminalize a -3-lawful act would give Sanctuary a different criminal code, which that clause forbids. III.V's own text conditions on "any qualifying breach" and its example is assault: it sorts breaches, it doesn't manufacture offenses; the single civilization-wide code (via the Consent Ceiling's above--3 scoping, laws.html:2347) defines no offense inside -3 custom, and the federal floor's two triggers don't reach it (laws.html:1812). The rating track is mandated in terms ("cannot commit acts in -3 and escape Sanctuary rating," laws.html:2330). The run's proposed one-sentence closure is accurate but **declaratory/redundant**. No canon change.

### S6 (friction — LP-079 forfeiture-band selection): **RESOLVED-BY-EXISTING-TEXT (premise was a misread)**
There are not two standing schedules — there is **one scale read from both ends**: 10% retained ≡ 90% forfeit. The CBA entry says visitors/elective residents transfer "on the same progressive scale" (laws.html:1497), and the whitepaper glossary is dispositive: "Voluntary permanent residents undergo full asset liquidation **using the same scale**" (whitepaper.html:1779). The permanent-residency "bands" are that scale applied full-estate at the one-time emigration filing — not an alternative channel for inbound value. An heir's inbound bequest rides the single authorized downward channel at the one scale, exactly as LP-079's singular "the standing forfeiture scale… down through the schedule" (law-polling.html:2570) already says. The run's 93%/7% number was correct; only its rationale (band-selection ambiguity) was wrong. The suggested CBA one-liner would be harmless but redundant. No canon change.
