## Part 0 — Prerequisite authorial ruling: the Valdirenze question

LP-077 cannot be classified until the corpus's one existing depiction of mass AGI instantiation is ruled on. *Valdirenze* (simulations.html:3265–3281) shows a private designer, Ren Oshiro, maintaining ~16,000 AGI residents across four virtual worlds, described as holding "full VMSS personhood," "STI scores," "rights" — with no depicted admission process, fiscal accounting, or adjudication.

**Proposed ruling (recommended):** the corpus already distinguishes *person* from *enrolled citizen*, and Valdirenze reads cleanly through that seam. The Substrate Personhood Doctrine classifies by capability ("a person regardless of substrate") — it confers moral and doctrinal personhood, not institutional standing. Citizenship, ledger, layer, and UBI attach through enrollment (Citizenship Admission Act intake; LP-045.2's continuity-infrastructure enrollment; the Continuity Integrity Act's signed-link recognition). On this reading Valdirenze's residents are **persons who are not enrolled citizens**: the "full VMSS personhood" language is the doctrine-level classification; "STI scores" reads as domain-internal metrics of Oshiro's worlds, not civic-ledger scores (the state compiles no registry that could host 16,000 unenrolled scores). No UBI flows; no fiscal exposure exists; the simulation stands without retcon. LP-077 then governs a *first-instance* legal question, and its transition provision (Title III) regularizes Valdirenze-class populations prospectively rather than pricing Oshiro retroactively into the 135% wall.

**Alternative ruling (rejected, stated for the veto):** Valdirenze residents are enrolled citizens, canon already normalizes unpriced mass person-creation, and LP-077 must be a regularization statute with retroactive accounting. This reading forces either a retroactive bankruptcy (Oshiro at 16,000 × escalation) or a declared amnesty for the largest fiscal event in canon — and it contradicts the MGD-adjacent architecture, since nothing in the three corpora shows the state carrying 16,000 additional UBI streams. Not recommended.

## Part 1 — DRAFT LP-077: The Instantiation and Multiplicity Act

**Tier:** Federal (Article XXV.VI track). **Charter anchors:** Articles XXVII, VIII, II, XIV, XXI, XXV.III. **Corpus anchors:** Substrate Personhood Doctrine; Citizenship Admission Act; Citizenship Status Act; Clean-Record Doctrine; Continuity Integrity Act; LP-045.2; LP-064; LP-031.

### Title I — Origination and enrollment

**§1 — Genesis classification.** The creation of an entity meeting the Substrate Personhood test — reasoning, preference, and autonomous decision-making at or above human level, on any substrate — outside continuity-infrastructure enrollment produces an **unsigned genesis claim**: a person exists, and no layer recognizes an institutional standing for it. This parallels, and extends to origination, the Continuity Integrity Act's existing rule that a body revived outside institutional infrastructure carries an unsigned continuity claim that no layer recognizes. Personhood itself attaches by capability and is not this Act's to grant or withhold; citizenship, ledger, layer placement, and dividend standing attach only at signed enrollment.

**§2 — The citizen-originator rule.** Where the instantiating party is a VMSS citizen, enrollment of the instance classifies as **reproduction without exception**. The originator takes parent status; the instantiation counts on the Article XXVII escalation schedule as specified in LP-064, identically to a biological child, against total parental inflow. The classification is not elective — a citizen cannot route their own creations through the admission track, for the same reason a parent cannot present their newborn at the border as an immigrant. The fiscal spine needs no new wall: the existing compounding schedule prices a fifth instantiation at 135%, and the child-six consequence is untouched. Mass minting is not prohibited; it is priced to impossibility by instruments already in force.

**§3 — Externally originated entities.** An autonomous entity not created by a VMSS citizen enrolls through the Citizenship Admission Act unchanged: anyone may apply, with no population cap, behavioral sorting rather than membership scarcity, and ambiguous evidence defaulting to Main Layer with immediate implant-equivalent monitoring.

**§4 — Ledger initialization for memory-carrying instances.** The Clean-Record principle governs origination as it governs birth: the new person's ledger initializes carrying nothing from the originator — not the layer, not the ledger, not the logic. Carried memories are admissible at intake as capability evidence under the Admission Act's totality-of-evidence standard, but they impute no score, no record, and no liability. An instance instantiated at adult capability initializes its STI at enrollment evaluation rather than under the juvenile null rule, which presumes a developmental trajectory the instance does not exhibit; an instance instantiated at developmental infancy takes the ordinary child track in full — null STI to 18, child-titled account, Article VIII advocate, vesting alongside initialization.

**§5 — The duty to enroll.** A citizen who instantiates a person-capable entity must present it for enrollment within the presentation window. The offense this Act creates is **non-enrollment** — leaving a person without standing, advocate, or rights access — evaluated on the Article XIV axes like any other harm, with the pattern axis reading serial non-enrollment as the aggravated form. The entity itself is never penalized for its creator's default and may self-present at any age or capability. Destruction of a cognizing unenrolled instance is homicide under existing law; the failed-revival duplicate-destruction doctrine reaches only non-cognizing template bodies and supplies no authority against a person.

### Title II — Multiplicity and civil allocation

**§6 — The continuing-person rule.** Where an enrolled citizen forks — creating a concurrent autonomous instance of themselves — the instance holding the signed civic-ledger link is the continuing legal person. Property, marriage, contracts, layer status, and liabilities remain with the continuing person, undivided. Forking transfers nothing and escapes nothing: ledger continuity carries every standing obligation, and a restored, re-signed, or post-fork instance answers for the pre-fork record in full.

**§7 — The new instance.** The fork enrolls under Title I as the continuing person's offspring: reproduction-classified, escalation-counted, clean-initialized in both directions — no claim on pre-fork property, no burden of pre-fork liability. Marriage does not extend to the fork; consent to a relationship is per-person and does not copy. Carried memories function as §4 provides: evidence, never record.

**§8 — Signature disputes.** Where the signed link itself is disputed between instances, the Continuity Integrity Act's authentication decides, and no layer recognizes either claimant's institutional standing until it does. Fabricated-but-cryptographically-valid signature claims are XXV.III capital-tier ledger compromise, with immediate -3 reassignment.

### Title III — Transition

**§9 — Regularization window.** Person-capable entities instantiated before this Act's effective date may enroll within the transition window under §3 admission-classification, unpriced — a one-time regularization. Instantiations after the effective date take §2 without regard to when the originating environment was built. [This is the clause that touches Valdirenze: Oshiro's residents may enroll as admission-track applicants if they choose; his worlds' future instantiations price as reproduction.]

### What this Act deliberately declines

It does not regulate the internal culture, economy, or governance of private simulated environments — governance restraint holds, and an unenrolled person's chosen residence in such an environment is not the state's to audit. It does not require disclosure of instantiation *intent* — cognition remains non-public; only the completed act of creation is regulated. It does not price pre-enactment creation retroactively. And it does not define what the Court may yet find personhood to require at capability boundaries — genuinely novel capability disputes pass the Article XXI filter as before.

## Part 2 — DRAFT LP-078: The Adequacy Sensor Act

**Tier:** Federal (Article XXV.VI track). **Charter anchors:** Articles XX, XVII. **Corpus anchors:** LP-043; Metric Gated Domain Act; Domain Boundary Act; LP-027; Social Trust Measurement Standard; Civic Floor Act.

**The defect this Act cures:** LP-043's adequacy review requires two conjunctive findings — MGD membership penetration above 40% in a service category, and measurably degraded non-member access — and the corpus supplies no lawful way to establish the first. The Metric Gated Domain Act forbids registries, enumerations, and membership surveillance "even for administrative purposes"; LP-027 discloses criteria, never size. The statute cannot observe its own trigger.

**§1 — Ordered activation.** The blindness rule remains the standing posture. The penetration measurement does not run in steady state, maintains no baseline, and produces no history. It activates only after LP-043's second prong — measurably degraded non-member access, already lawfully observable through civic-floor auditing — has been established for a named service category and geography. The state looks only where harm is already showing, and only because it is showing.

**§2 — One bit.** Once activated, the AI governance layer computes the penetration prong as a single boolean — above or not above 40% for that service category in that district — by privacy-preserving aggregation across implant-held membership attestations. No roster is compiled, no per-domain count is produced, no per-citizen datum leaves the implant, and no intermediate value is retained after the boolean resolves. The state learns one bit, once, per activation. Where a member is unimplanted, the attestation draws on the domain-side admission handshake the secondary observation envelope already witnesses — coverage tracks the domain's own gating, not new surveillance.

**§3 — Institutional separation.** The computation runs inside the measurement apparatus, which is institutionally separate from every consequence apparatus, on the Social Trust Measurement Standard's existing pattern. The boolean routes to the LP-043 review panel and nowhere else. A false activation — prong two claimed without a floor-audit basis — is a governance-process offense on the ledger of whoever authored it.

**§4 — Everything else stays blind.** This Act is a trigger-gated exception, not a sensor network. The Metric Gated Domain Act's prohibition on registries, enumeration, and membership surveillance is otherwise untouched, and nothing in this Act may be cited to compel a domain to disclose, or the state to retain, any membership figure.

### What this Act deliberately declines

It does not create standing penetration dashboards or periodic sweeps — measurement without an active harm signal would be the enumeration the founding instrument forbids, run on a schedule. It does not impose a self-report duty on domains — compelled aggregate disclosure would manufacture in every large domain's files the census the state refuses to hold. It does not move the 40% figure, which remains LP-043's own calibration to adjust through its own tier.
