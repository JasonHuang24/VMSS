### S1 ORGANIC — Twenty-six hours in a coolant plant
**Scenario:** Mara, a Main Layer resident, works one qualifying critical-infrastructure job maintaining a district coolant loop. Her contract pays a freely negotiated base wage of $20 per hour. During a warm week she works twenty-six hours: twenty ordinary qualifying hours and six additional hours. Her ledger shows the hours, the job's qualifying designation, and which entity requested them. She lives in rented housing and has no unusual status. The question is whether an otherwise consensual low-wage bargain can waive the civilization's time dividend. It cannot. VMSS has no minimum wage, so the $20 bargain stands, but the six hours above twenty engage a separate employer-funded entitlement. Payroll corrects the line item before settlement; Mara's UBI and PJS remain untaxed.

**Governing instruments:**

1. Charter Art. III.II — market wages are free, but every qualifying hour beyond twenty triggers the employer-funded Overtime Premium Protocol.
2. The Overtime Premium Protocol (LP-076) — Main's rate is $125 per additional hour, rounded before multiplication.
3. Charter Arts. III.I and III.III — Main UBI is $10,000 monthly; UBI and PJS are untaxed without exception.

**Ruling:** The employer owes $750, not $60, in overtime premium, in addition to the agreed $520 wage. The erroneous template is corrected; no punitive consequence follows absent knowing evasion.

**Verdict:** COVERED.

**Confidence:** high — I am least sure whether an innocent payroll error receives any private ledger notation, because the cited rules fix the entitlement but not clerical-correction procedure.
