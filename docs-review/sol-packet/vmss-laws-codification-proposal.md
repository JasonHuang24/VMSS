## 0. Verdict

**The mapping proposes zero changes. All 48 Charter units keep their designations.**

That is the finding, not a failure to find one. The governing rule in §18.1 is that unchanged is the
default and the burden is on change, and after a four-lens survey, a three-refuter adversarial pass
over every proposal, and a completeness critic, **not one unit meets the burden**. The structural
reason is a single sentence, and it is worth stating plainly because it is the honest justification
for a null result:

> **No Charter title ever named a schedule, so removing the schedules orphaned no title.**
> All 46 numbered headings name a mechanism, a principle, or a subject — *Primary Job Subsidy*,
> *Currency Siloing*, *Savings Circulation Mandate*. Not one contains the words *rate*, *band*,
> *schedule*, *threshold*, *window*, or *percent*. LP-076 removed magnitudes that no title had ever
> advertised, and each of the five enabling grants restates the retained principle inside the same
> sub-article the schedule left (charter.html:190, :196, :201, :209, :235, :419). The document that
> LP-076 produced is the document its own headings already described.

**Consequences of the null mapping, each of which is a decision for Jason:**

1. **The concordance (§2) is empty.** There is no old→new pair to record.
2. **The citation partition (§3) has an empty actionable set.** All 1,562 corpus occurrences of
   Charter designations stay as written. The partition is still delivered in full, because it is the
   *pricing* that proves the cost of every change the run declined.
3. **The guard-retarget census (§4) has no retarget to perform** — but it surfaced two live guard
   gaps and one unguarded invariant that exist regardless of LP-077, and those are reported.
4. **LP-077 should not be filed (§5).** A codification amendment with an empty schedule is an
   Article XI filing that amends nothing. The draft is supplied anyway, conditionally, because §18.1
   reserves the instrument design to Jason and he may ratify a change this run declined.
5. **The cleanup work is real, and none of it is Charter-tier (§6).** Every item in the inventory
   lives on a live surface below the Charter. Under LP-042 that is consolidation and reference
   hygiene, not amendment. **If the mapping stands, the entire L3b cleanup costs no in-world event
   at all** — it is an editorial pass, not constitutional history.

That last point is the run's most valuable output. The commission assumed a codification would need
an amendment. On the evidence, it does not.

### 1.3 The one proposal that got traction, and why it still fails

**XXII.II is a genuine orphan numeral.** This is verified, not disputed: `charter.html:329` is the
only sub-article in the Charter whose series begins at `.II`, and **no `XXII.I` exists anywhere in
the corpus** (strict grep, whole tree). Article III starts at III.I, Article XXV at XXV.I, Article
XXVIII at XXVIII.I. XXII is the sole exception.

Two cures were proposed and both were adversarially tested:

**Cure A — renumber XXII.II → XXII.I.** Refuted 3/3, and by the strongest refuter on the plainest
ground: the Charter's other subdivided articles are *not* structurally identical to XXII, so the
claimed convention ("the identical structure elsewhere starts at .I") is false at source. Article
XXV's lead text at :369 is a one-paragraph preamble to six sub-articles; Article XXII's lead text at
:324–328 is **five paragraphs of operative rule** — the Meritboard's entire constitutional
definition. XXII is not a mis-started series; it is an article with a substantive body plus one
appendix. Renumbering the appendix to `.I` would assert that the body is not a unit, which is a
construction claim about what Article XXII contains, not a designation fix.

**Cure B — designate the lead text as a new XXII.I.** Survived the three refuters 2–1, then was
**refuted by the completeness critic on a cost nobody had priced**, which I verified independently:

> **Article XXII is the only article in the Charter with article-level closing prose positioned
> *after* its final sub-article.** `charter.html:336` — *"The same civilization that trusts the STI
> ledger to sort 4.3 billion residents into layers trusts measurable achievement to sort its
> leadership…"* — is a paragraph about the Meritboard's breadth, not about the review cycle. Today
> it reads as Article XXII's closing. Designate the lead text `XXII.I` and that paragraph is
> silently absorbed into `XXII.II`, whose subject it is not. **That is text moving between units —
> explicitly out of scope.**
>
> Verified: Article III ends at :240, Article XXV closes its pillar box at :410, Article XXVIII at
> :458 — all three end *at* their last sub-article with no trailing article-level prose. There is no
> precedent shape to fall back on.

Cure B carries a second unpriced cost: the lead text at :324–328 contains no self-naming phrase, so
any `XXII.I` title must be **coined** — canonical-sounding vocabulary invented by a run with no
authoring licence, and a designation no instrument enacted.

**Disposition: XXII.II is UNCHANGED.** The orphan is recorded here as a known, deliberate
irregularity so that a future run does not rediscover it and mistake it for an unexamined defect.
If Jason wants it cured, Cure B plus an explicit relocation of `:336` is the only coherent form —
and the relocation makes it Article XI substance, not codification.

### 4.2 The two pins that actually cost money

**(1) `check-canon.mjs:972` — Tier-1 title byte-equality.** Compares `charter.html`'s h2 inner HTML
against `laws.html`'s `code-index-title` text after `.trim()` only. That includes the designation
prefix, the **U+2013 en dash with one space each side**, and raw entities (`&amp;` in 11 headings).
*Retarget:* none — the guard derives both sides. The **cost** is a same-commit rewrite of the
matching `laws.html` index title for every retitle, entity-for-entity and dash-for-dash.

**(2) `check-canon.mjs:1207` + `:1223` — anchor resolution**, across 55 external + 49 in-page
references. *Retarget:* none. The project-consistent cure for an anchor that must change is an
**alias** — keep the old id alive on a separate empty element beside the retargeted heading so frozen
records keep resolving. Caveat: the alias interacts with the duplicate-id guard at `:1166`, so the
old id must live on the alias element **only**, never also on the heading.

### 4.4 Three gaps found — real regardless of LP-077

**G1 — the Charter's own table of contents is unguarded, and it restates all 30 titles.**
This is the census's most important finding and I verified it myself.

`charter.html:104–141` is a **hand-authored** `<div class="law-toc" id="charterToc">` with 30
`toc-link` rows, each carrying a `toc-num` numeral and a `toc-txt` title. I verified programmatically
that **all 30 `toc-txt` values are currently exact suffix copies of their matching h2 titles — zero
mismatches.** The invariant holds. **Nothing enforces it:**

- **No generator.** `build-law-toc.mjs` never opens `charter.html` — its `toc-num`/`toc-txt` emitters
  at `:78` and `:189` serve `law-polling.html` and `laws.html` only. There are no `BEGIN`/`END`
  markers in `charter.html`.
- **No guard.** Every `charter.html` reference in `check-canon.mjs` is at `:558`, `:615`, `:715`,
  `:960`, `:962`, `:1145`, `:1162`, `:1232`, and **none reads `toc-txt` or `toc-num`.** The §9 guard
  at `:1232` pins only the TOC's *hrefs*.

**Consequence:** a retitle that correctly updates the heading and the `laws.html` index ships **fully
green with 30 stale strings on the Charter's own front page.** Add ~60 strings (30 titles + 30
numerals) to the same-commit cost of any change. Also unguarded: `charter.html:106`'s hardcoded hint
*"Preamble · 28 articles · Founding Affirmation"*. By contrast the `laws.html` Tier-1 TOC rows are
generated and self-heal — which is probably why nobody looked for a second, hand-authored one.

**Recommendation (independent of LP-077): add a guard asserting the 30 `toc-txt` values equal the h2
title suffixes.** The invariant is already true; pinning it costs one check and closes a silent-drift
class on the Charter itself. This is a guard *addition*, not a retarget, and it is out of L3a's
docs-only scope — flagged for Jason as a standalone item.

**G2 — sub-article designations are essentially unguarded.** 17 of the 18 sub-articles carry **no
`id`** and appear in no guard. Renumbering `III.VIII → III.VII`, or retitling any sub-article h3,
would pass the entire toolchain green except for the vacuous `:1158` pin. Whatever is concluded about
sub-article misfit, **CI will not catch a mistake there.**

**G3 — visible designation prose is unguarded.** The link-integrity guards pin the *href*
`#article-xxv-vi`; nothing pins the visible text *"Article XXV.VI"* inside those anchors at
`charter.html:190, :196, :209, :235, :290` and `laws.html:293, :681, :687, :1397`. A renumber could
land every href correctly and leave nine retired designation strings on live surfaces, green. A live
instance already exists: `laws.html:813` renders *"Article III.VII"* as the visible label of an
`href="charter.html#article-iii"` anchor, because sub-articles have no ids of their own.
