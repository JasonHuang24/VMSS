Lower Layers & Consequences

What layer reassignment means in practice and how the lower rings differ from one another.

Self-chosen refusal: Rare, but respected. A citizen can decline backup vessel revival at any point — the result is permanent death. The decision is logged, honored, and irreversible. No penalty attaches to the choice itself. Most residents choose revival. Those who do not have made a sovereign decision about their own continuity, and the civilization treats it accordingly.

Refusal after committing harm: A perpetrator who self-terminates after an offense does not escape consequence — the act is recorded on the implant ledger before death. The record stands permanently. Revival is not forced. If the perpetrator had pre-registered a revival refusal or declines during the revival window, that choice is honoured. The victim receives full restoration — backup vessel revival, medical treatment, therapy, and any applicable restitution — regardless of the perpetrator's survival. The system does not need the perpetrator alive to deliver justice. The ledger is the consequence. Death does not erase it.

Coerced refusal: A third party who pressures, manipulates, or financially induces someone into refusing revival has crossed the harm threshold. This is not protected speech. The real-world analog is coercion into suicide — pressuring a revivable person to remain dead is functionally equivalent to encouraging a living person to die. The coercer faces layer reassignment: -1 for financial inducement, social pressure, or one-time persuasion attempts (non-violent coercion); -2 for sustained psychological campaigns, exploitation of grief or vulnerability, organized pressure from multiple parties, or prolonged targeted manipulation of someone in a compromised state. The boundary is the same one that separates -1 from -2 across the entire system: non-violent offense versus violence-equivalent offense. Keeping someone dead through sustained psychological coercion is violence-equivalent — the outcome is a corpse that could have been a living person.

Visitation & Cross-Layer Movement

Cross-layer commerce is legitimate, common, and bounded by the visitor protocols. An -1 client who needs technical work performed in -3, a Main Layer specialist willing to take a contract in a layer below their own for premium pay, a -3 cooperative that needs an outside specialist for a job its internal labour pool cannot supply — all of these find each other through private brokers who operate primarily in -1 and maintain reputation-based networks across layers. The brokers are sometimes called fixers in the layer's commercial vernacular. They are private capital and contract allocators, accountable through personal trust and the market-association ledgers their networks rely on, charging a standing brokerage percentage on the transactions they arrange.

The cross-layer dimension follows the standard visitor protocols. The operator who travels to perform the work travels as a visitor — vessel link active (unless they have separately chosen to suspend it), federal surveillance floor recording, return path intact. The work is performed. The operator returns. The payment is released through the broker's escrow. The federal floor watches the broker's ledger at the standing surveillance level applied to all -1 commercial activity, which is sufficient to detect any contract that would cross the federal thresholds. The brokers are not criminals. They are the connective tissue between layers that the doctrine left deliberately unscripted, and the connective tissue is what allows the cross-layer economy to function without the doctrine having to centrally plan it.

There are three distinct mechanisms. Downward visitation is temporary — you retain your origin layer status, your origin layer assets, and your full institutional relationship with the civilization. No liquidation occurs. You arrive economically neutral in the destination layer and participate in the local economy by earning local currency through work. Elective residency is an indefinite commitment — you choose to live in a lower layer, retain your origin-layer status and assets, and may return at any time. No liquidation occurs. Voluntary permanent residency is a permanent change of status — you file formally, your assets are liquidated per the progressive schedule, the upward pathway closes, and your institutional relationship is restructured. Visitors and elective residents can leave whenever they choose. Voluntary permanent residents have sealed themselves in.

Four tiers, each with its own modification instrument. Charter (Article XI) contains structural principles and layer placement criteria — the founding core. The routing test: does the proposed instrument control who goes to which layer? Yes → Article XI. No but cross-layer → XXV.VI. Within-layer operational → XXVIII. Each tier's rewriting cost is calibrated to the structural weight of what is being changed — Charter amendment is the most expensive because it touches the foundations; regulatory change is the least expensive because it touches daily operations.

The Charter is not the civilization's prohibition list. It contains structural principles, layer placement criteria, and constraints on what instruments beneath it can do. Substantive prohibitions — including serious ones like murder, fraud, and assault — live at Article XXV (federal law), one tier below. The parallel with Earth's best-known founding document is exact. The US Constitution criminalizes almost nothing substantively. Murder, theft, fraud, rape, assault, kidnapping — none appear in the constitutional text. They live in 18 USC and state penal codes. The Constitution's job is structural: separation of powers, enumerated rights, amendment procedure. Treason is the one substantive criminal reference, and it appears in Article III Section 3 specifically to constrain how Congress can define it — a protective ceiling against political weaponization, not a substantive floor. VMSS applies the same principle. Murder is prohibited through Article XXV.VI at the federal tier, calibrated through a drafting ladder that allows prohibitions to be added, refined, or repealed in response to new threats without reopening the founding document. The Charter protects the architecture from majoritarian erosion. XXV carries the prohibition list.

This matters because "amend the Charter to ban X" is nearly always the wrong instrument — the same way "amend the US Constitution to ban X" is nearly always the wrong instrument on Earth. The right tier for new substantive prohibitions is federal law. The Charter reserves its structural weight for the architecture itself.
