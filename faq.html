Lower Layers & Consequences

What layer reassignment means in practice and how the lower rings differ from one another.

Self-chosen refusal: Rare, but respected. A citizen can decline backup vessel revival at any point — the result is permanent death. The decision is logged, honored, and irreversible. No penalty attaches to the choice itself. Most residents choose revival. Those who do not have made a sovereign decision about their own continuity, and the civilization treats it accordingly.

Refusal after committing harm: A perpetrator who self-terminates after an offense does not escape consequence — the act is recorded on the implant ledger before death. The record stands permanently. Revival is not forced. If the perpetrator had pre-registered a revival refusal or declines during the revival window, that choice is honoured. The victim receives full restoration — backup vessel revival, medical treatment, therapy, and any applicable restitution — regardless of the perpetrator's survival. The system does not need the perpetrator alive to deliver justice. The ledger is the consequence. Death does not erase it.

Coerced refusal: A third party who pressures, manipulates, or financially induces someone into refusing revival has crossed the harm threshold. This is not protected speech. The real-world analog is coercion into suicide — pressuring a revivable person to remain dead is functionally equivalent to encouraging a living person to die. The coercer faces layer reassignment: -1 for financial inducement, social pressure, or one-time persuasion attempts (non-violent coercion); -2 for sustained psychological campaigns, exploitation of grief or vulnerability, organised pressure from multiple parties, or prolonged targeted manipulation of someone in a compromised state. The boundary is the same one that separates -1 from -2 across the entire system: non-violent offense versus violence-equivalent offense. Keeping someone dead through sustained psychological coercion is violence-equivalent — the outcome is a corpse that could have been a living person.

The cooperative that accepts payment to permit a profitable enterprise is not betraying its mandate — its mandate is to serve its district's interests as the cooperative defines them, and accepting payment for non-interference is exactly the mandate functioning as designed. The cooperative that endorses an enterprise outright is doing the same thing through a different channel. The cooperative that simply ignores an enterprise that has not generated complaints is doing it through the laziest channel of all. None of this is anarchy. It is a different regulatory regime, with the regulator local, purchasable, and operating on its own incentive structure rather than on a Charter-imposed floor. The federal floor — clean energy, nuclear weapons, implant integrity, civilizational sovereignty — still bounds what no cooperative is permitted to authorise.

It would be structurally worse. Earth remaining outside the system is not a failure of adoption — it is an architectural feature. VMSS requires an external destination for citizens who voluntarily exit under Article X. Without one, the exit provision — the civilization's most confident structural statement — becomes meaningless. A system you cannot leave is a prison regardless of how well it treats you.

Earth also serves as the de facto sorting ground for the unsorted. The 5.7 billion people who did not join at founding are not VMSS's problem to classify. If the civilization covered the entire planet, every human alive would require layer assessment, and -3 Terminal would absorb a vastly larger population than the architecture was designed for. The current model — a sovereign civilization coexisting with an external world — keeps the sorting voluntary, the exit real, and the layer populations proportional to the infrastructure that serves them.

VMSS does not aspire to planetary coverage. It aspires to being so obviously superior that alliance becomes the rational choice for external nations — while remaining a choice, not a mandate.

Visitation & Cross-Layer Movement

Cross-layer commerce is legitimate, common, and bounded by the visitor protocols. An -1 client who needs technical work performed in -3, a Main Layer specialist willing to take a contract in a layer below their own for premium pay, a -3 cooperative that needs an outside specialist for a job its internal labour pool cannot supply — all of these find each other through private brokers who operate primarily in -1 and maintain reputation-based networks across layers. The brokers are sometimes called fixers in the layer's commercial vernacular. They are private capital and contract allocators, accountable through personal trust and the market-association ledgers their networks rely on, charging a standing brokerage percentage on the transactions they arrange.

The cross-layer dimension follows the standard visitor protocols. The operator who travels to perform the work travels as a visitor — vessel link active (unless they have separately chosen to suspend it), federal surveillance floor recording, return path intact. The work is performed. The operator returns. The payment is released through the broker's escrow. The federal floor watches the broker's ledger at the standing surveillance level applied to all -1 commercial activity, which is sufficient to detect any contract that would cross the federal thresholds. The brokers are not criminals. They are the connective tissue between layers that the doctrine left deliberately unscripted, and the connective tissue is what allows the cross-layer economy to function without the doctrine having to centrally plan it.

Voluntary descent is permanent change of status. Voluntary residents have changed their address in a meaningful sense.

Yes. By the time AGI reaches the threshold of general intelligence, classifying it as anything other than a person is incoherent. This extends to governance. The Supreme Court's flexible composition — human, AI, AGI, cyborg, in any ratio — reflects the same principle. The civilization that measures trust and competence does not restrict its highest institutions to a single substrate.

A merit-based executive structure. The President is the chief executive, drawn from and selected by the Meritboard — a body of 50 to 100 members representing the civilization's highest achievers across every major discipline. The Meritboard certifies competence for the highest executive roles and serves as the talent pool from which the President and presidential deputy are drawn. Supreme Court justices are drawn from the AI governance candidate pool — structurally independent from the executive branch. The President appoints justices from that pool; the Meritboard holds audit authority over AI governance; the Court checks both through constitutional rulings. The Supreme Court of 10 justices handles edge cases where automated governance and existing doctrine produce ambiguous outcomes. No elections, no constituencies, no campaigns. Competence is measured, not voted on. Full governance structure in the Charter.

Through petition, expert drafting, and direct population ratification — no legislators, no representatives, no campaigns. Any citizen can initiate a regulatory petition within their layer. Once signatures reach 1% of the layer's population (roughly 30 million in Main Layer, scaling proportionally in other layers), the Meritboard assigns a domain-expert panel to draft the regulation. The drafted law goes to a direct population vote — every resident of the affected layer votes, no representatives vote on their behalf. 80% ratification enacts it as binding law, enforced by the AI governance system. The same mechanism operates at district level for localized issues — districts are defined as one million residents, boundaries redrawn annually by the AI governance system using real-time population data. No gerrymandering is structurally possible. In -3 Terminal, enacted regulations are advisory only — consistent with institutional withdrawal. Full mechanism in Article XXVIII.

Economic consequence, not criminalization. Article XXVII establishes a replenishment tax that activates at the fifth child per household. The first four children carry no additional burden. Starting at the fifth, a 50% compounding tax increase restructures the household's fiscal position per additional child. By child five, the tax burden makes upper-layer economic life unsustainable for most households — descent follows from arithmetic, not punishment. By child six, the burden is catastrophic. Children are held completely harmless: full UBI, full legal advocacy, standing right to relocate to Main Layer autoparenting at any age. The penalty targets the reproductive decision, never the people produced by it.

A post-scarcity civilization that guarantees institutional support to every citizen cannot permit unlimited population growth without degrading those guarantees. The replenishment tax makes this constraint self-enforcing. Full mechanism in the Charter.

A revived species carries the same ecological and legal protections as a surviving one. Deliberately driving a species to extinction and then commercially reviving it as a novelty product or luxury attraction does not produce a "net positive" — the original destruction remains on the responsible parties' ledgers regardless of subsequent remediation. Remediation is commendable. It does not retroactively nullify documented harm.

This prevents "extinction-as-investment-strategy" — the deliberate destruction of a species followed by commercial de-extinction marketed as conservation. This is an intended feature of longevity, not an unintended consequence. When people live for centuries, grievances that would have died with the aggrieved party on Earth persist in living memory. A person wronged in 2050 is still alive in 2350, still remembers, and still carries the emotional weight of that event. Multi-century grudges between families, between communities, between former associates — these are real and expected.

The system's response is institutional, not suppressive. VMSS does not mandate forgiveness or force reconciliation. The tools are available. Their use is voluntary. A citizen who carries a 300-year grudge is not penalised for it. A citizen who acts on that grudge by crossing a behavioural threshold faces the same consequence as anyone else.

Longevity amplifies everything — including pain. The civilization built the therapeutic infrastructure to match the timescale. Whether a given citizen uses it is their choice. What they do with their unresolved feelings is governed by the same behavioural thresholds that govern everyone else.
