-1 Behavioral Noncompliance Layer

Low-Harm Offense Stratum • Civic Violation Tier

The -1 Behavioral Noncompliance Layer is the first true step downward in The Five Rings Civilization. It is the entry point into the Hell Layers — a place where residents have committed low-harm offenses (theft, harassment, chronic lying, moderate torment, repeated antisocial behavior). The layer is still structured enough to have basic automated support, but noticeably harsher than Main Layer (0): reduced abundance, lower trust, and more friction in daily life.

-1 exists as a warning and a filter. It is not the terminal abyss of -3, but it is far from the safety of Main. Most residents here are still capable of redemption in their own eyes — they can maintain some semblance of order — but the path back upward is closed. The layer serves as a living lesson in consequence: small breaches accumulate, and the cost becomes tangible.

Key Characteristics

  • UBI & Economy: $5,000/month UBI + $5,000/month Primary Job Subsidy (one job, ≥20 hrs/week). Still sufficient for basic needs, but tighter. Layer-specific Compliance Token (CPT) currency dominates; barter and grey-market economies are common.
  • Enforcement Model: Post-Intervention — harmful acts may complete; victims are revived/treated via backup vessels + automated therapy. Perpetrators risk further descent to -2 or -3 if acts escalate.
  • Environment: Greyer, more crowded, visible tension. Buildings show wear — recycled materials, dimmer lighting, occasional barricades. Public spaces are functional but lack the openness of Main.
  • Monitoring & Safety: Minimal AI tracking (logs only, no preemptive intervention). Ambient drones exist but are fewer and slower to respond than in Main or Heaven. Trust is low — STI halos are mostly orange/red.
  • Population & Demographics: Mix of residents who descended for repeated low-level violations (theft, harassment, chronic deception). Some are still hopeful of proving themselves; others have accepted their place.
  • Daily Infrastructure: Automated drops for basic rations, water recyclers, power cells. Informal markets and barter hubs. No free fabricators — goods must be earned or traded.

A Day in the Life of a -1 Resident

Let's follow Marcus Hale, a 34-year-old former graphic designer who descended 18 months ago after repeated small-scale fraud and harassment (chronic lying to clients, dodging debts).

Marcus wakes in his thin-walled concrete room — no smart glass, no sunrise simulation. He manually cranks the opacity down on a small window slit. Dashboard check: $5,000 UBI credited, STI 42 (red flag visible to all). He feels the weight immediately — no gentle wake-up, no abundance buffer.

Breakfast is a nutrient pack from the automated drop slot — bland, functional. No flavor printers. He eats standing, then heads to the district market — a chaotic bazaar of repurposed containers and stalls. He barters yesterday’s labor (fixed a neighbor’s water purifier) for fresh fruit and protein bars. Currency is CPT — digital but clunky, with high transaction friction. Everyone’s STI halo is orange/red — no greens. Trust is low; deals are tense, eyes always scanning for deception.

Mid-morning: “maintenance rounds” — informal gig fixing solar panels, water lines, hacked tech. Pay: 300–500 CPT per job. He avoids the rougher crews — they’re the ones edging toward -2 with escalating violence. He keeps his head down. One wrong move (theft from another resident, assault) and he drops to -2. The implant reminds him constantly: “Intent detected → threshold approaching.” It’s not fear; it’s math.

Lunch alone at a communal bench — calorie-dense paste, no flavor. Conversation with two others: both descended for similar patterns (harassment, chronic lying). They talk Main Layer nostalgia — effortless abundance, trust. One mentions trying to game a voluntary descent to escape debt; now regrets it. STI halos flash red when lies surface — no hiding here.

Afternoon: quiet hour in a ruined atrium. He pulls an old Main Layer memory clip (private archive): a day walking in sunlight, no tension. The contrast hurts. He exits feeling heavier. Evening: small fire in a sealed room (power rationed). They share stories — mostly pre-descent regrets. No comfort. Just endurance.

Bedtime: mattress on the floor. Knife in hand. Distant shouts. Sleep is shallow — always half-expecting a raid or fight. Tomorrow will be similar: survive, pay tribute, don’t slip.

Enforcement in Practice

In -1, post-intervention applies. Serious harm (murder, severe assault) can complete if the perpetrator ignores warnings. Victims are revived via backup vessel in -1 medical bay. Perpetrators risk further descent to -2 or -3. Evidence is captured via implant/AR recordings + AI reconstruction. The layer is still "insulated" enough that revival is reliable, but safety is reduced — gangs and vigilante justice fill the gaps.

Edge Cases & Unique Aspects

  • Voluntary Visitors from Main: Can return unless they breach while visiting (then locked in -1 or lower).
  • Children Born Here: Automatically relocated to Main autoparenting upon birth.
  • Bailout: Available after minimum torment duration — revives in -1 with restrictions (reduced UBI, intensified STI monitoring).
  • Internal Crimes: Escalating violence or murder → -2 or -3 reassignment.
  • STI Visibility: Low trust makes cooperation hard. High-STI individuals are rare and often targeted for exploitation.
  • Reconstruction Drift: Rare personality/memory shifts upon revival add psychological realism even in -1.

-1 is the warning shot. It is the first real taste of consequence — reduced abundance, eroded trust, and the constant shadow of further descent.
